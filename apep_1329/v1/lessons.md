## Discovery
- **Idea selected:** idea_2205 — UK FIT triple-threshold bunching. Chosen for spectacular data (860K installations with exact capacity), bunching method's strong tournament track record (24.5 avg), and the natural experiment from the 2016 band merger.
- **Data source:** Ofgem FIT Installation Report (3 Excel files, ~100MB) — URLs needed web search to locate; data.gov.uk CKAN endpoint was 404.
- **Key risk:** Post-merger polynomial counterfactual might not capture distribution shift, making formal $\hat{b}$ unreliable for the pre/post comparison.

## Execution
- **What worked:** Raw bunching ratios are spectacular and tell the story cleanly. Year-by-year dynamics (10:1 → 1,410:1 → 1.4:1) are the strongest evidence. Placebo comparison (tariff vs non-tariff round numbers) works decisively.
- **What didn't:** Polynomial bunching estimates are insensitive to the threshold removal — $\hat{b}$ stays at ~38 post-merger. This is a well-known limitation of polynomial counterfactuals in small, structurally different samples. Had to be transparent about this.
- **Review feedback adopted:** (1) Recharacterized from kinks to notches throughout — the FIT applies a single rate to ALL output, making threshold crossings a discrete revenue drop. (2) Added transparent discussion of why polynomial estimates fail post-merger. (3) Toned down "most dramatic ever" rhetoric. (4) Caveatted welfare calculation as illustrative.
