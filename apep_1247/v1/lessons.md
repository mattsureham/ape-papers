## Discovery
- **Idea selected:** idea_1519 — ARRA Pell Grant Bartik design for racial enrollment at community colleges
- **Data source:** IPEDS DuckDB (reused from prior APEP paper infrastructure) — race category harmonization across 2008 IPEDS transition was the main data challenge
- **Key risk:** Great Recession confound; pre-ARRA Pell share correlates with recession exposure

## Execution
- **What worked:** The Bartik framework is clean and novel for this setting. Black enrollment share outcome is more interpretable than log levels. The "composition lever" framing gives the mechanism a portable name.
- **What didn't:** Pre-trends in the Black share event study undermine causal identification. White enrollment "placebo" failed badly. Hispanic results contaminated by IPEDS race reclassification around 2008. State-level clustering drops significance to marginal.
- **Review feedback adopted:** All three reviewers flagged the same issues — softened causal language, added caveats about inference, de-emphasized log-level results, framed as descriptive/suggestive evidence. The paper is honest about what it can and cannot show.
