## Discovery
- **Idea selected:** idea_0561 — Staggered UK opt-out organ donation, testing the canonical Johnson & Goldstein default-effect hypothesis within a single healthcare system
- **Data source:** NHSBT Annual Activity Reports — publicly available PDFs with nation-level breakdowns, required manual compilation from multiple years' reports
- **Key risk:** Only 4 clusters (nations), limiting statistical power. Addressed with RI and leave-one-out sensitivity.

## Execution
- **What worked:** The paradox framing — consent up, transplants not — is the paper's strongest asset. The mechanism data (48% deemed consent authorization) is powerful and comes directly from NHSBT PDA Chapter 13. The living donor placebo is a clean falsification test.
- **What didn't:** Annual nation-level data limits the panel to 40 observations. Monthly data would strengthen the design substantially but requires more extensive data compilation from NHSBT quarterly reports.
- **Review feedback adopted:** Added estimator choice justification (why TWFE over CS DiD), MDE discussion, transplant magnitude explanation, and Callaway-Sant'Anna citation. Reviewers correctly noted that the few-cluster setting limits any single estimator's credibility — the paper's contribution is the paradox pattern, not the precise causal magnitude.
