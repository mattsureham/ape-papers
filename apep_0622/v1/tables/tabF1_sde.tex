{\footnotesize \emph{Notes:} This table reports standardized effect sizes (SDE)
to facilitate cross-study comparison of treatment effect magnitudes.
SDE $= \hat{\beta} / \text{SD}(Y)$ for binary treatment.
SD($Y$) is the unconditional standard deviation of the outcome from the full sample
(Table~\ref{tab:summary}), before conditioning on fixed effects.

\textbf{Research question:} Does state adoption of EV-specific registration fees reduce electric vehicle adoption?
\textbf{Treatment:} Binary indicator for state having enacted an EV registration fee.
\textbf{Data:} DOE/AFDC Experian vehicle registrations, 50 states, 2016--2023.
\textbf{Method:} Staggered DiD with Callaway--Sant'Anna estimator, state-clustered SEs.
\textbf{Sample:} 400 state-year observations; 32 treated states, 18 never-treated.

Classification thresholds:
large negative ($< -0.15$), moderate negative ($-0.15$ to $-0.05$),
small negative ($-0.05$ to $-0.005$), null ($-0.005$ to $0.005$),
small positive ($0.005$ to $0.05$), moderate positive ($0.05$ to $0.15$),
large positive ($> 0.15$).
Classification labels refer to the magnitude of the standardized point estimate,
not to statistical significance. ``Null'' denotes a near-zero effect size
($|$SDE$| < 0.005$), not a failure to reject a null hypothesis.}
