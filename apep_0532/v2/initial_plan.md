# Initial Research Plan — apep_0532 v2

## Research Question
Does extreme weather increase climate awareness in India, and does agricultural dependence mediate the weather-to-attention channel?

## Identification Strategy
State-month panel with state and year-month FE. Temperature anomalies (relative to 30-year normals) interacted with pre-determined (Census 2001) agricultural employment shares.

## Data Sources
1. Google Trends: climate, agricultural, placebo search terms (21 states, 2004-2024)
2. NASA POWER: gridded temperature and precipitation (0.5° × 0.5°, 1981-2024)
3. Census of India 2001: agricultural employment shares
4. TRAI: internet penetration by state
5. GDELT: supplementary news coverage analysis

## Key Analyses
1. Primary OLS: temperature anomaly × agricultural share → climate search
2. Attention substitution: same specification with agricultural/placebo search outcomes
3. Seasonal split: monsoon vs non-monsoon
4. Internet heterogeneity: high vs low penetration states
5. Placebo tests: lead weather, placebo outcomes
6. Robustness: alternative specifications, sample restrictions

## Expected Contribution
Boundary condition for experiential learning about climate: the mechanism is mediated by economic structure. Attention substitution as behavioral signature.
