# Reply to Reviewers — Round 1

## Reviewer 1 (GPT-R1)

**"Agricultural interaction captures correlated state traits, not identified mechanism"**
We agree that the interaction is observational heterogeneity consistent with, but not definitively proving, agricultural crowd-out. We moderated all mechanism claims in the abstract, introduction, and conclusion. The paper now frames results as "suggestive evidence of heterogeneous responses by agricultural structure."

**"Wild cluster bootstrap needed with 22 clusters"**
Added. WCB p-values are 0.127 (level) and 0.105 (log) for the interaction. Reported prominently in Section 6.1 with honest discussion that results are suggestive.

**"Capital-city weather is too crude"**
Acknowledged as a limitation. Using gridded state averages would require substantial data infrastructure beyond this paper's scope. We note the measurement error concern more prominently.

**"Lead regressions need joint specification"**
Added joint lead-lag model. Leads are jointly significant (F=4.72, p=0.009) conditional on contemporaneous temperature, confirming imprecise timing. Discussed honestly as a limitation of monthly frequency data. Reframed leads as timing diagnostics, not placebos.

**"Horse-race interactions with other state characteristics"**
Deferred to revision. This is the most valuable improvement for a subsequent version.
