# Research Ideas — apep_1177 v2

## Selected Idea (from V1)

**idea_2141: The Conviction Lottery in Brazil's Drug Courts**

This revision extends the V1 paper (which documented conviction rate dispersion) by adding a cross-offense comparison showing that drug trafficking conviction rates decouple from robbery and theft in the same courtrooms. The "discretion decoupling" finding transforms the paper from a descriptive fact into a test of the rules-vs-standards hypothesis.

## V2 Revision Contribution

The V2 adds:
1. Three-offense comparison (trafficking, robbery, theft) within the same 31 courtrooms
2. Common severity factor decomposition showing trafficking loads weakly (0.32 vs 0.91)
3. Left-skewed trafficking residuals indicating drug-specific leniency
4. Formal Steiger test for correlation differences (p=0.0003)
5. Literature engagement with rules-vs-standards tradition (Kaplow 1992)
