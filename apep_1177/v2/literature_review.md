# Early Literature Novelty Check — apep_1177 v2

## Novelty Assessment

The V2 contribution — showing that drug trafficking conviction rates decouple from robbery/theft in the same courtrooms, with a formal common-severity-factor decomposition — has no direct precedent in the literature. Key check:

1. **Frandsen, Lefgren & Leslie (2023 AER)**: Tests monotonicity/exclusion in judge designs but does not compare cross-offense correlation structures.
2. **Coulibaly et al. (2024)**: Sharp tests that reject leniency validity for drug categories in Stevenson's data — relevant methodological concern but different setting.
3. **Assunção & Trecenti (2023)**: Study Brazilian drug court variation but do not compare to other offense types.
4. **No paper directly compares conviction/sentencing dispersion across offense types with different statutory determinacy within the same court system.** The comparison-offense test is genuinely novel.

## Conclusion

The paper's main contribution — discretion decoupling as an empirical test of rules-vs-standards — is novel and fills a gap identified in the literature scan.
