**2. Summary**  
The authors exploit cross‑county variation in pre‑reform OxyContin brand share as a quasi‑experimental shock to isolate the effect of the 2010 abuse‑deterrent reformulation on county‑level labor‑market outcomes (employment, earnings, hires, separations). Using a continuous‑treatment DiD design on 2,619 U.S. counties (2005‑2019), they find no statistically significant negative impact; coefficients are small, sometimes slightly positive, and age‑group heterogeneity tests suggest the instrument captures broader economic trends rather than opioid‑specific channels. The paper concludes that the reformulation instrument is valid for mortality outcomes but fails the exclusion restriction for labor‑market outcomes.

**Overall Assessment**  
The paper tackles an important question with an innovative data set and a plausible quasi‑experimental design. The null finding is interesting, but the current identification argument is insufficiently rigorous to rule out alternative explanations. Strengthening the first‑stage linkage, expanding falsification tests, and addressing the exclusion restriction more directly will be essential for the paper to meet AER‑Insights standards. With those revisions, the manuscript would make a valuable methodological contribution (the “instrument boundary” lesson) and a substantive contribution (clarifying the limits of supply‑side opioid reforms for labor‑market outcomes).
