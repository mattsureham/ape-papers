\item \textit{Notes:} \footnotesize \textbf{Country:} United States. \textbf{Research question:} Does SNAP Emergency Allotment termination increase formal labor supply among low-income workers? \textbf{Policy mechanism:} Eighteen states voluntarily terminated SNAP EA supplements (\$95--\$250/month) between April 2021 and mid-2022, cutting household income for SNAP recipients. \textbf{Outcome definition:} Log quarterly new hires (HirN) from Census QWI, measuring the flow of workers into formal employment. \textbf{Treatment:} Binary; state terminated Emergency Allotments in a given quarter. \textbf{Data:} Census Quarterly Workforce Indicators (QWI), 2019Q1--2023Q4, 992 state-quarter-race observations. \textbf{Method:} TWFE with state and quarter fixed effects; standard errors clustered at the state level. \textbf{Sample:} 50 U.S.\ states, 20 quarters; 17 early-terminating states as treated, 33 as controls. SDE $= \hat{\beta} / \text{SD}(Y)$ where SD($Y$) is the unconditional standard deviation. Classification refers to magnitude, not statistical significance: Large ($|$SDE$| > 0.15$), Moderate ($0.05$--$0.15$), Small ($0.005$--$0.05$), Null ($< 0.005$).
