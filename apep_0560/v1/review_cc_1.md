# Internal Review — Round 1

## Decision: Minor Revision

## Summary

This paper examines stock market contagion from tailings dam failures using an event study methodology with 118 events from the WISE Chronology (1996-2025) and 42 global mining firms. The core finding is that peer firms operating tailings dams suffer 0.79 pp larger CARs than streaming/royalty firms without tailings exposure, and this differential intensified after the 2020 GISTM. The aggregate mean CAR is positive but statistically insignificant with event-clustered SEs (t=0.61), so the paper's main contribution is the cross-sectional heterogeneity.

## Strengths

1. **Novel research question** — first systematic study of market contagion from tailings dam failures across the global mining sector
2. **Comprehensive robustness** — two-way clustering, leave-one-out, triple-period specification, placebo tests, XME benchmark
3. **Honest reporting** — clear about the insignificant aggregate effect, frames heterogeneity as the main finding
4. **Strong identification** — the streaming/royalty comparison group provides a clean within-mining-sector contrast

## Issues

1. **Minor:** The three-firm control group (WPM, FNV, RGLD) is acknowledged but remains a structural limitation
2. **Minor:** Binary tailings measure is coarse — continuous exposure would strengthen results
3. **Minor:** Event dating from WISE may not match market revelation dates precisely

## Verdict

Paper is methodologically sound, honestly reported, and makes a genuine contribution. Ready for external review.
