# Late Literature Positioning Audit

## Paper: apep_0589 — When the Subsidy Stops
## Method: Sharp RDD at EU 75% GDP/capita threshold

## Key Human Papers Cited

1. **Becker, Egger, & von Ehrlich (2010, 2013, 2018, 2023)** — Core EU threshold RDD papers studying the *positive* effects of receiving Objective 1 status. Our paper inverts this by studying the *negative* effects of losing eligibility.

2. **Garcia-Mila & Ponce (2023)** — West German Zonenrandgebiet subsidy persistence. Key comparison: they find persistent positive effects after termination; we find convergence stalling.

3. **Criscuolo, Martin, Overman & Van Reenen (2019)** — UK Regional Selective Assistance. Firm-level persistence of subsidized employment.

4. **Rodriguez-Pose & Fratesi (2004), Rodriguez-Pose & Garcilazo (2015)** — Institutional quality and cohesion policy effectiveness.

5. **Boldrin & Canova (2001)** — Early skepticism about EU structural fund effectiveness.

6. **Barca (2009)** — Place-based policy reform proposal.

## Overlap Risk Assessment

- **Overlap with Becker et al.:** LOW-MEDIUM. They study receipt; we study withdrawal. Different margin, different estimand.
- **Overlap with Pellegrini et al. (2013), Giua (2017):** LOW. They use similar thresholds but study different outcomes and periods.
- **Overlap with Cerqua & Pellegrini (2017):** LOW. They study 2007-2013 effects.

## Delta Statement

This paper's unique contribution is estimating the *withdrawal* margin of EU cohesion policy — what happens when regions lose eligibility — rather than the *receipt* margin studied in all prior threshold RDD papers. The event study and manufacturing channel provide novel evidence on the dynamics and mechanisms of subsidy dependence.

## Literature Gaps Remaining

The paper could benefit from:
- More engagement with the "benefit cliff" literature in US social policy
- Recent work on spatial spillovers of EU cohesion spending
- Dynamic RD methods literature (Cattaneo, Idrobo, Titiunik 2020)
