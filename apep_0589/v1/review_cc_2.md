# Internal Review - Round 2

## Summary
This paper studies the economic consequences of losing EU structural fund eligibility using a sharp RDD at the 75% GDP/capita threshold. The design is clean and the institutional setting is compelling. The main estimate (-7.0 pp GDP convergence decline, p=0.17) is economically large but imprecise. The event study provides stronger evidence of gradual divergence.

## Critical Issues
1. The main RDD estimate does not reach conventional significance (p=0.17). The paper handles this honestly but it remains the primary limitation.
2. Donut specifications show sign reversal at ±2-3pp, which the paper explains as small-sample artifact.
3. The parametric specifications yield much smaller estimates, suggesting sensitivity to functional form.

## Strengths
- Excellent institutional knowledge and background section
- Clean identification with proper validity checks
- Event study is compelling and well-executed
- Manufacturing mechanism is theory-driven, not ad hoc
- Honest calibration of claims to evidence

## Suggestions
- A formal first-stage showing the ERDF payment discontinuity would strengthen the mechanism argument
- Consider fuzzy RDD treating ERDF intensity as the endogenous variable
- The comparison with Becker et al. magnitudes is valuable and should be expanded

## Assessment
The paper makes a genuine contribution by studying the withdrawal margin of EU cohesion policy. While the main estimate is imprecise, the combined evidence (RDD + event study + manufacturing channel) tells a coherent story. Suitable for a field journal or AEJ: Economic Policy.

DECISION: MINOR REVISION
