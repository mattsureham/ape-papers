# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: Mexico's Sembrando Vida Tree-Planting Subsidy and the Deforestation Paradox
**Policy:** Mexico's Sembrando Vida agroforestry program (launched January 2019) pays MXN 5,000/month to farmers planting fruit/timber trees on 2.5-hectare plots. Eligibility restricted to municipalities with medium-to-very-high social marginalization. Land must be "available" (not already forested), creating perverse incentive to clear forest. Rolled out across 20+ states, 995 municipalities by 2021, expanded to 23 states and ~451,665 beneficiaries by 2023. Budget grew from 15B pesos (2019) to 27B pesos (2020). Three treatment cohorts: 2019 original, 2020 Golden Triangle expansion (Chihuahua, Sinaloa), 2021 further expansion.
**Outcome:** Hansen/UMD Global Forest Change dataset (v1.12, 2001-2024): 30m resolution annual tree cover loss, downloadable from Google Cloud Storage (GeoTIFF tiles). Municipality-level zonal statistics via R (terra/sf) or Google Earth Engine. Secondary: CONEVAL rezago social index, Sembrando Vida beneficiary counts from Bienestar portal.
**Identification:** Callaway-Sant'Anna DiD with three sources of variation: (1) cross-municipality treatment timing (2019-2021 cohorts), (2) within-treated beneficiary density (dose-response), (3) 18 years pre-treatment data (2001-2018) for parallel trends validation. Never-treated municipalities serve as controls. Robustness: SA estimator, Rambachan-Roth HonestDiD sensitivity analysis, placebo treatment at 2015, heterogeneity by ecosystem type.
**Why it's novel:** Only one prior causal study exists (Perez Ponciano & Rojas 2025, CIDE) covering Chiapas only with standard TWFE (vulnerable to negative weighting). This paper provides: 23-state national coverage, heterogeneity-robust CS-DiD estimator, dose-response specification, 18 pre-treatment years, and ecosystem-level heterogeneity. WRI documented 73,000 hectares of forest loss in Sembrando Vida areas in 2019 alone, but only descriptively.
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: ~995 treated vs ~1,400 control municipalities; 3 cohorts with staggered rollout; Hansen/GFW satellite tiles downloadable (HTTP 200 verified, 30m resolution, 2001-2024); not overstudied (only one prior paper on single state).
