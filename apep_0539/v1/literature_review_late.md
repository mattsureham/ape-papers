# Late Literature Positioning Audit

## Key Papers Cited
1. Wright et al. (2017) — Missouri county-level EBT and burglary (direct predecessor)
2. Callaway & Sant'Anna (2021) — Primary estimator
3. Goodman-Bacon (2021) — Bacon decomposition, TWFE bias
4. Sun & Abraham (2021) — Interaction-weighted estimator
5. Becker (1968) — Economics of crime framework
6. Foley (2011), Tuttle (2019), Carr & Packham (2019) — SNAP timing and crime
7. Rogoff (2017) — Cashless society and crime
8. Muralidharan et al. (2016) — Cashless transfers in developing contexts

## Potential Gaps Identified by Reviewers
- Roth (2022) on pre-test issues in event studies — not cited, relevant for pre-trends discussion
- Rambachan & Roth (2023) on HonestDiD — referenced in code but not prominently in text
- Payment timing and crime literature (broader than just SNAP)

## Assessment
The literature coverage is adequate for the paper's scope. The main contribution (national scaling of a single-state finding) is clearly positioned relative to Wright et al. The methodological literature (CS-DiD, Sun-Abraham, Bacon) is well-cited. The Roth (2022) pre-test paper could be added in a future revision for completeness.

## Overlap Risk: LOW
No other paper appears to test EBT adoption on crime using national staggered DiD with modern estimators.
