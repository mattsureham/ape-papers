{\footnotesize \emph{Notes:} This table reports standardized effect sizes (SDE) to facilitate cross-study comparison of treatment effect magnitudes. SDE $= \hat{\beta} / \text{SD}(Y)$ for binary (0/1) treatment. SD($Y$) is the unconditional standard deviation of the age-standardised alcohol-specific mortality rate per 100,000 across all units and years in the regional panel (11 units $\times$ 11 years = 121 observations).

\textbf{Research question:} Does minimum unit pricing (MUP) for alcohol reduce alcohol-specific mortality?
\textbf{Treatment:} Binary; MUP in force (Scotland from 2018, Wales from 2020; England never treated).
\textbf{Data:} OHID Fingertips (England regions), NRS (Scotland), ONS (Wales), 2013--2023, annual.
\textbf{Method:} Two-way fixed effects (TWFE) and Callaway--Sant'Anna staggered DiD, cluster-robust SEs.
\textbf{Sample:} 11 units (9 English regions + Scotland + Wales) $\times$ 11 years = 121 observations.

Classification thresholds (7 categories): large negative ($< -0.15$), moderate negative ($-0.15$ to $-0.05$), small negative ($-0.05$ to $-0.005$), null ($-0.005$ to $0.005$), small positive ($0.005$ to $0.05$), moderate positive ($0.05$ to $0.15$), large positive ($> 0.15$). Classification labels refer to the magnitude of the standardized point estimate, not to statistical significance. ``Null'' denotes a near-zero effect size ($|\text{SDE}| < 0.005$), not a failure to reject a null hypothesis.}
