## Discovery
- **Idea selected:** idea_0131 — Japan's 301-employee disclosure threshold as RDD for gender equality
- **Data source:** MHLW Women's Active Engagement Enterprise Database (61,711 firms, open CSV)
- **Key risk:** Binned running variable prevents standard nonparametric RDD

## Execution
- **What worked:** Massive first stage (75pp disclosure jump), huge sample, clear asymmetric result
- **What didn't:** MHLW site requires session cookies for download (403 without); exact employee counts unavailable (only size bins)
- **Key insight:** Adjacent-bin comparison confounds mandate with firm-size gradient; parametric RDD separates them, revealing opposite signs for management (+3.8pp) vs wage gap (null)
- **Review feedback adopted:** Added LATE interpretation clarity, quadratic robustness mention, Japan-specific structural wage gap context, cross-sectional limitation discussion, international comparison with Norway quotas
