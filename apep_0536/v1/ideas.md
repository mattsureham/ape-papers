# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: Fiber to the Home, Polarization, and the Demand for Unreliable Information: Evidence from France's Broadband Rollout
**Policy:** France's Plan France Très Haut Débit (2013-present) created a staggered rollout of FTTH across departments, with deployment timing driven by zoning/institutional constraints (zones très denses, AMII, RIP) and the planned copper-network decommissioning (scheduled in lots by Orange from 2023). Treatment timing varies sharply across departments and is institutionally determined by zone classification and copper-closure lot assignment.
**Outcome:** Two outcome families: (1) Political polarization from official election results — vote shares for Rassemblement National and La France Insoumise, turnout, blank/null votes, vote dispersion (effective number of parties). Elections: Presidential (2012, 2017, 2022), Legislative, European, Municipal, Regional. (2) Information environment from GDELT — counts of local news articles mentioning conspiracy/misinformation terms and fact-checking brands (e.g., "désinformation", "complotisme", "Les Décodeurs", "CheckNews"), geolocated to French departments.
**Identification:** Staggered DiD (Callaway-Sant'Anna) exploiting within-department variation in FTTH coverage over time. Unit and time fixed effects. Event-study with flexible pre-trend diagnostics. Treatment: (a) continuous FTTH coverage rate, (b) binary threshold crossings (25%, 50%, 75%), (c) entry into copper-closure lot. Plausibility strengthened by: (i) showing pre-treatment political trends do not predict rollout timing within comparable deployment zones, (ii) using zone classification (RIP vs AMII vs private) as a source of quasi-exogenous timing variation, (iii) copper-closure lot assignment as an independent instrument. Triple-difference: within-zone × across-time × treated vs not-yet-treated.
**Why it's novel:** While Campante et al. (2018) study broadband and political participation in Italy and Falck et al. (2014) study internet and voting in Germany, no study exploits France's unique FTTH+copper-closure institutional setting. The dual treatment (fiber expansion + copper decommissioning) and the GDELT-based information-environment outcomes are novel. The ambiguous ex ante sign makes the result informative regardless of direction.
**Feasibility check:** ARCEP publishes quarterly department-level FTTH coverage data (open data, data.gouv.fr). Election results are available from data.gouv.fr with commune-level granularity. GDELT 2.0 provides event-level data with geographic coding queryable via BigQuery or bulk download. Copper-closure lot schedules are published by ARCEP/Orange. All data are public, no authentication needed for core sources. 96 metropolitan departments × ~40 quarters (2013-2023) = ~3,840 department-quarter observations. Feasibility: HIGH.

## Idea 2: Does Fiber Broadband Change Local News Consumption Patterns? Evidence from France's Digital Infrastructure Transition
**Policy:** Same FTTH rollout under Plan France Très Haut Débit, focusing on how bandwidth improvements change the mix of local vs national news consumption.
**Outcome:** Google Trends indices for local newspaper names vs national media brands at the regional level; GDELT article counts geolocated to French departments distinguishing local vs national sources.
**Identification:** Staggered DiD exploiting differential FTTH rollout timing across departments. Event-study design with department and quarter fixed effects.
**Why it's novel:** Tests whether broadband expansion crowds out local journalism — a mechanism that could explain political effects. Complementary to Idea 1 but narrower.
**Feasibility check:** Google Trends regional data for France is available but extraction at department level is unreliable. GDELT source-level data could distinguish local vs national but requires extensive coding. Feasibility: MODERATE — geographic granularity of media consumption data is the binding constraint.

## Idea 3: Broadband Infrastructure and Municipal Fiscal Behavior: Does Digital Connectivity Change Local Public Finance in France?
**Policy:** Same FTTH rollout, focusing on whether connectivity improvements change municipal tax bases, spending patterns, or fiscal capacity.
**Outcome:** Municipal budget data from DGCL/data.gouv.fr — tax revenues, investment spending, operating expenditure, debt levels. Available at commune level annually.
**Identification:** Staggered DiD using commune-level FTTH availability from ARCEP against commune-level fiscal outcomes. Event-study with commune and year fixed effects.
**Why it's novel:** The fiscal channel of broadband is understudied. Could work through: (i) property values (broadband capitalization), (ii) firm location decisions, (iii) telework-induced residential sorting.
**Feasibility check:** Municipal fiscal data is published annually on data.gouv.fr. Commune-level FTTH data from ARCEP is available but may require aggregation from sub-commune reporting. Feasibility: MODERATE — the outcome data is clean but the mechanism is diffuse and may require long horizons.
