# Late Literature Positioning Audit — apep_0536/v1

## Paper Title
Fiber to the Home and the Rise of Anti-System Politics: Evidence from France's Broadband Rollout

## Core Contribution
First paper to estimate the political effects of second-wave broadband (FTTH) using France's staggered deployment across 96 departments, with outcomes spanning 11 elections (1999-2024).

## Key Related Human Papers

### Broadband & Politics (Direct Competitors)
1. **Campante, Durante & Sobbrio (2018)** — "Long-Range Effects of Broadband Infrastructure" (AEJ:Applied). Studies US broadband and elections. Our paper extends to FTTH (not DSL) in a different institutional context (France).
2. **Lelkes, Sood & Iyengar (2017)** — "Hostile Audience" (AJPS). US broadband access and partisan affect. We study anti-system voting rather than affective polarization, and use a different technology generation.
3. **Falck, Gold & Heblich (2014)** — Internet and electoral accountability (EER). Cross-country analysis; our paper exploits within-country staggered rollout.

### Broadband & Institutions
4. **Guriev, Melnikov & Zhuravskaya (2021)** — "3G Internet and Confidence in Government" (QJE). Studies 3G rollout across 116 countries. Our paper focuses on fiber (not mobile) in a single developed democracy with rich election-level outcomes.
5. **Boxell, Gentzkow & Shapiro (2017)** — Greater internet use not associated with faster polarization growth (PNAS). Demographic decomposition approach; our paper uses quasi-experimental variation.

### Econometric Methods
6. **Callaway & Sant'Anna (2021)** — CS-DiD estimator (JoE). Used as robustness check.
7. **Sun & Abraham (2021)** — Heterogeneous treatment effects in event studies (JoE). Applied as robustness.
8. **de Chaisemartin & D'Haultfœuille (2020)** — TWFE bias with heterogeneous effects (AER). Cited for methodological context.
9. **Roth (2022)** — Pre-test conditioning (AER:Insights). Cited for placebo test interpretation.
10. **Borusyak, Jaravel & Spiess (2024)** — Revisiting event-study designs (ReStud). Cited for imputation approach.
