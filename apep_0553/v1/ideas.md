# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: The Sanctions Sieve: Product-Level Rerouting of Controlled Technology to Russia Through Transit Countries
**Policy:** Western sanctions on Russia (Feb-Mar 2022) banned exports of dual-use technology, electronics, and precision machinery. The EU, US, UK, and Japan subsequently created the Common High Priority Items List (CHPL) in May 2023, expanded Sep 2023 (+7 HS codes) and Feb 2024 (+5 CNC codes, total 50 HS6 codes). CHPL targets items found in Russian weapons systems (Kalibr cruise missiles, Kh-101 missiles, Orlan-10 UAVs).
**Outcome:** UN Comtrade bilateral trade data via public API (comtradeapi.un.org). Annual data at HS4/HS6 level, reporter x partner x product x year. FOB export value, net weight, quantity. Coverage 2015-2024 (10 years), 200+ reporters. Mirror statistics approach: transit countries' reported exports to Russia (since Russia stopped reporting after 2021).
**Identification:** Triple-difference (DDD). First diff (time): pre-sanctions (2015-2021) vs post (2022-2024). Second diff (product): CHPL-listed (50 HS6) vs non-CHPL sanctioned products in same HS2 chapters. Third diff (geography): transit countries (Kyrgyzstan, Kazakhstan, Armenia, Turkey, UAE, India) vs neutral non-sanctioning countries with low Russia trade. Beta_1 measures rerouting; beta_2 measures CHPL enforcement effect (expected negative). Built-in placebo: non-CHPL products in same HS2 chapters.
**Why it's novel:** No published paper evaluates CHPL enforcement via product-level Comtrade data. Egorov et al. (2024, CEPR) use confidential Russian customs data — not replicable. Scheckenhofer et al. (2025, AEA P&P) examine aggregate military goods without HS6 product variation. First publicly replicable product-level evidence on sanctions circumvention and enforcement effectiveness.
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: Kyrgyzstan HS 8542 exports to Russia rose 2,300x (2021-2023), then fell 90% by 2024 (CHPL enforcement). Comtrade API confirmed accessible without authentication. 50 CHPL products x 15 transit countries x 10 years = 7,500+ cells. No existing APEP papers on trade/sanctions.

## Idea 2: When the Canal Runs Dry: Trade Costs and Port Diversion from the 2023-24 Panama Canal Drought
**Policy:** The 2023-24 drought at the Panama Canal forced a 50% reduction in daily ship transits (36→18) from July 2023 through February 2024 due to El Nino-driven low water levels. Restrictions eased gradually, with full capacity restored by August 2024. The drought was driven by weather, not policy — a pure supply shock.
**Outcome:** Monthly port-level merchandise imports from US Census Bureau International Trade Data API. Covers ~100 US customs districts/ports, monthly, by country of origin and HS commodity code. No API key required.
**Identification:** Continuous treatment DiD: East Coast/Gulf ports (30-50% Canal-dependent for Asian imports) vs West Coast ports (direct trans-Pacific, zero Canal dependence). Port x origin double-difference absorbs port and country shocks. West Coast ports should show *increased* volumes (diversion test).
**Why it's novel:** No econometric paper on the 2023-24 Panama Canal drought. Feyrer (2021, REStud) studies 1967 Suez closure at country-pair level. Panama drought is unique: exogenous weather, recent, granular monthly data, clean East/West Coast contrast, gradual and temporary (recovery dynamics observable).
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: Census port-level import API returns data by port, country, HS code. ~35 ports x 18 months. El Nino timing exogenous. Houthi Red Sea crisis (Nov 2023) primarily affects Europe-Asia, not Asia-US routes.

## Idea 3: The Environmental Cost of Downstreaming: Air Pollution from Indonesia's Nickel Export Ban
**Policy:** Indonesia banned export of unprocessed nickel ore in two phases: partial ban Jan 2014, total ban Jan 2020. Forced domestic smelter construction (43+ smelters with captive coal-fired power plants) concentrated in Central/Southeast Sulawesi and North Maluku.
**Outcome:** Annual mean PM2.5 from SEDAC Global Annual PM2.5 Grids (V5.GL.04), 1998-2022, at ~1km resolution. Secondary: MODIS AOD at 1km daily, VIIRS nightlights for industrial activity.
**Identification:** Staggered DiD comparing ~17 nickel-producing districts (treated) vs ~500 non-nickel districts (control). Nickel deposits geologically determined (laterite on ultramafic rock). CS-DiD estimator. Placebo: bauxite districts; spatial decay analysis; wind direction analysis.
**Why it's novel:** No causal environmental study of Indonesia's nickel ban exists. Existing work (CEPR 2024) evaluates employment effects only. First welfare-relevant pollution cost estimate enabling cost-benefit analysis of resource nationalism.
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: ~17 treated districts, ~500 controls, 25 years of PM2.5 data. SEDAC freely downloadable. Only 17 treated units is small N — needs RI and leave-one-out robustness.
