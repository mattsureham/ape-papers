# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: The Credential Cliff: Multi-Cutoff RDD Estimates of Matric Pass-Level Effects on Tertiary Access and Employment in South Africa
**Policy:** South Africa's National Senior Certificate (matric) exam assigns learners to three hierarchical pass levels — Higher Certificate, Diploma, and Bachelor's — based on subject-score thresholds (30%/40%/50%) applied across 7 subjects. Each level unlocks a distinct tier of tertiary education. The cutoffs are fixed nationally since 2008 and applied mechanically with no discretion.
**Outcome:** (1) Tertiary enrollment within 1-3 years (HEMIS university enrollment records); (2) Tertiary completion within 6 years; (3) Employment status and monthly earnings (NIDS panel waves 1-5, 2008-2017; QLFS quarterly cross-sections); (4) Occupational quality (ISCO codes mapped to skill levels).
**Identification:** Multi-cutoff sharp RDD following Cattaneo, Titiunik & Vazquez-Bare (2020). Running variable: the binding-constraint subject score at each threshold (30%, 40%, 50% in the relevant subject). The design is sharp — pass-level assignment is purely mechanical from subject scores. Three cutoffs create built-in placebos: the Diploma/Higher Certificate boundary serves as a placebo for the Bachelor's/Diploma boundary (and vice versa). Bandwidth selection via Calonico, Cattaneo & Titiunik (2014) with bias-corrected robust CIs. Full robustness battery: McCrary density tests, covariate balance, donut-hole RDD, alternative bandwidths, polynomial specifications, heterogeneity by gender/province/school quintile.
**Why it's novel:** No RDD paper exists on South Africa's matric thresholds despite ~400,000 passers/year. Multi-cutoff with built-in placebos across three credential tiers. Massive sample (~2.8M observations, 2010-2016). High-stakes context: 30%+ unemployment, matric pass type is the single most important credential in the South African labor market. Complements Pop-Eleches & Urquiola (2013, Romania) and Hastings, Neilson & Zimmerman (2013, Chile) but studies credential-tier access rather than school assignment.
**Feasibility check:** Variation confirmed — three fixed cutoffs applied mechanically since 2008. Data access confirmed — DataFirst hosts NSC Examination 2010-2016 microdata (DOI: 10.25828/pcn8-pc32, licensed access); NIDS 5 waves publicly available; QLFS via Stats SA. Novelty confirmed — no published RDD on matric pass-level thresholds. Sample size: ~2.8M passers + ~1.5M failures. Manipulation concern LOW — centralized marking by trained markers, Umalusi external moderation applies uniform adjustments.
