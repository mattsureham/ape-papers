- **Selected idea title:** The Credential Cliff: Multi-Cutoff RDD Estimates of Matric Pass-Level Effects on Tertiary Access and Employment in South Africa
- **Why this is not duplicative:** No NBER or published paper applies RDD to South Africa's matric pass-level thresholds. Pop-Eleches & Urquiola (2013) study school assignment RDD in Romania; Hastings, Neilson & Zimmerman (2013) study field-of-study returns in Chile. Neither studies credential-tier access in a three-level system.
- **Specific contribution delta (1-2 sentences):** This paper exploits three fixed, mechanically-assigned credential cutoffs (30%/40%/50%) in South Africa's matric exam — the first RDD on this system — to separately identify the returns to Higher Certificate, Diploma, and Bachelor's access in a 30%+ unemployment economy. The multi-cutoff design with ~400K passers/year and built-in cross-cutoff placebos provides identification distinct from any existing study.
