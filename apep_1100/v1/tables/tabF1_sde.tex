\textbf{Country:} Italy.
\textbf{Research question:} Does lottery-assigned grading court identity explain geographic disparities in bar exam pass rates?
\textbf{Policy mechanism:} Annual sorteggio (public lottery) assigns grading commissions across Courts of Appeal within size tiers.
\textbf{Outcome definition:} Candidate court pass rate on the esame di stato per l'abilitazione all'esercizio della professione di avvocato.
\textbf{Treatment:} Identity of the lottery-assigned grading court (varies year to year for each candidate court).
\textbf{Data:} Ministry of Justice sorteggio verbales (grading court assignments) merged with pass rates from legal news aggregators (FormazioneGiuridica, Money.it). Court-year panel, 2017--2023.
\textbf{Method:} Variance decomposition and OLS with candidate-court and year fixed effects. Leave-one-out grading court leniency (raw and FE-residualized). Heteroskedasticity-robust standard errors.
\textbf{Sample:} 93 court-year observations across 26 Courts of Appeal and 4 exam sessions. Unbalanced panel (25, 24, 24, 20 courts per session).
Classification refers to magnitude, not statistical significance.
