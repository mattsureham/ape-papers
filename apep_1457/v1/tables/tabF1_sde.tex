\begin{tablenotes}[flushleft]
\footnotesize
\item \textit{Notes:} Classification refers to magnitude of the point estimate, not statistical significance. ``Large positive'' denotes economically meaningful bunching ($\hat{b} > 0.10$). ``Moderate positive'' denotes detectable but smaller bunching ($0.05 < \hat{b} < 0.10$). ``Zero'' denotes no economically meaningful bunching or bunching in the opposite direction.
\item \textbf{Country:} United States
\item \textbf{Research question:} Does the 2014 Farm Bill's Supplemental Coverage Option create bunching at the 75\% coverage level in federal crop insurance?
\item \textbf{Policy mechanism:} SCO eligibility and enterprise unit subsidies create a price kink at 75\% coverage, making additional coverage beyond 75\% disproportionately expensive.
\item \textbf{Outcome definition:} Excess mass ratio $\hat{b}$ at 75\% coverage, measuring the fraction of policies above the counterfactual density.
\item \textbf{Treatment:} 2014 Farm Bill introduction of SCO (difference-in-bunching: post-2014 vs.\ pre-2014).
\item \textbf{Data:} USDA RMA Summary of Business files (sobcov), 2000--2023, 20.7 million policy-years.
\item \textbf{Method:} Bunching estimation (Kleven and Waseem 2013) with polynomial counterfactual; bootstrap SEs.
\item \textbf{Sample:} Buy-up coverage for corn, soybeans, wheat, cotton across 2,230 U.S.\ counties.
