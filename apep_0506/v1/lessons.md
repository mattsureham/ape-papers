## Discovery
- **Policy chosen:** India mandatory candidate affidavit disclosure — unique RDD setting with wealth data
- **Ideas rejected:** Drought RDD (studied), Banking inclusion (needs SHRUG), SC/ST reservation (too close to Pande 2003)
- **Data source:** DataMeet election archive + MyNeta affidavit data. Major challenges: Indian rupee string parsing, name matching (49.4% rate), state name harmonization
- **Key risk:** Match rate attrition and whether mechanical wealth jump without downstream outcomes is sufficient

## Review
- **Advisor verdict:** 3 of 3 PASS (GPT errored on credits)
- **Top criticism:** Both referees flagged sample selection from 49.4% match rate
- **Surprise feedback:** Grok noted vanishing premium is descriptive, not RDD-identified
- **What changed:** Softened causal language, added Dutta & Gupta and Eggers & Hainmueller citations, improved opening hook

## Summary
- **What went well:** Clean RDD with exhaustive robustness; striking pattern (60% → 50%)
- **What could improve:** Need downstream outcomes (nightlights downloaded but not integrated); match rate limits external validity
- **For future revisions:** Integrate nightlights, add formal match rate analysis, obtain incumbency data
