    *   *Before:* "• Spillovers. ZRR loss in some communes..."

2.  **Punch up the Abstract:** The abstract is a bit heavy on citations (Rambachan and Roth). Keep the focus on your finding. Move the sensitivity caveat to the final sentence or omit the author names from the abstract to maintain narrative flow.
3.  **Refine the "Comparison Group" section:** In Section 8.3, you discuss the "gainer-vs-never" comparison. Use more Glaeser-esque language here: "Comparing a dying rural village to a booming tech suburb tells us little about the effect of a tax credit."

5.  **Strengthen the transition to Section 7:** The transition from Results to Mechanisms is a bit abrupt. End Section 6 with a sentence that explicitly invites the "Why?" question.
