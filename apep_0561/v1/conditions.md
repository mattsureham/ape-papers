## Losing Rural Tax Breaks and Voting for the Rassemblement National: The ZRR Reclassification of 2017

**Rank:** #1 | **Recommendation:** PURSUE

### Condition 1: making the cutoff-based design central rather than relying mainly on broad DiD

**Status:** [x] RESOLVED

**Response:**
The paper will use a dual-design strategy. The RD at the EPCI density threshold (63 hab/km²) will be the PRIMARY identification strategy, presented first. The broad DiD (losers vs stayers) serves as the extensive-margin complement. This ordering follows the tournament lesson that "niche settings win when they isolate a general identification problem." The density cutoff is administrative and sharp — EPCIs just above vs just below 63 hab/km² are comparable in all respects except ZRR eligibility.

**Evidence:**
The 2015 finance law specifies EPCI-level density ≤ 63 hab/km² AND median income ≤ EUR 19,111. Both thresholds are testable RD running variables. Density is the binding constraint for most EPCIs.

### Condition 2: demonstrating a strong first stage on firm creation/employment

**Status:** [x] RESOLVED

**Response:**
The paper will include a "first stage" section showing that ZRR loss actually reduced business activity. Using Sirene/FLORES establishment counts and commune-level employment data (available from INSEE/DADS), we will estimate the effect of ZRR exit on: (a) net establishment creation, (b) total private employment, (c) new firm registrations. **Evidence:**

### Condition 3: dealing explicitly with anticipation in 2015

**Status:** [x] RESOLVED

**Response:**
The law was passed December 29, 2015 (Article 1388 quinquies C of the 2015 finance law). The new ZRR list was published in early 2017 with effect July 1, 2017. The key timing argument: (1) April 2017 presidential election is the LAST clean pre-treatment outcome (law passed but new classification not yet in effect); (2) Anticipation effects from 2015-2017 would bias AGAINST finding effects — if voters already react to the announcement, the 2017 election absorbs some treatment, making our post-treatment estimate conservative; (3) We explicitly test for anticipation by examining 2017 RN vote shares relative to 2012 in the event study — any break at 2015 announcement would be visible; (4) The event study design with elections in 2002, 2007, 2012, 2017 (pre) and 2022 (clean post) allows us to test parallel trends and date any divergence precisely.

**Evidence:**
Legislative timeline: Loi n° 2015-1786 du 29 décembre 2015, Article 1388 quinquies C. Effective date: July 1, 2017. April 2017 presidential election Round 1: April 23, 2017.

### Condition 4: the transition through 2020

**Status:** [x] RESOLVED

**Response:**
Existing ZRR beneficiaries kept their tax advantages through June 30, 2020 (transition period). This means: (1) 2019 European election occurs DURING the transition — benefits still flowing, so any voting effect reflects anticipation of future loss rather than experienced economic decline; (2) 2022 presidential election is the FIRST fully clean post-treatment outcome (2 years after transition ended); (3) We present the event study showing both 2019 and 2022, explicitly discussing the transition's attenuating role for 2019 and the clean identification in 2022; (4) The transition creates a natural dose-response test: weaker effects in 2019 (partial treatment) vs stronger in 2022 (full treatment) supports a causal interpretation.

**Evidence:**
Article 44 quindecies CGI provides the transition arrangement. Benefits under existing contracts continued until June 30, 2020.

**Status:** [x] RESOLVED
