- **Selected idea title:** Combined Ideas 1+3: "What's in a Label? Education Priority Zones, Private School Escape Valves, and the Housing Market Value of School Assignment in France"
- **Why this is not duplicative:** Fack & Grenet (2010, JPUBE) is the closest existing work — boundary RDD for school quality → housing in Paris using 1997-2004 notarial data. Our paper differs on: (1) National scale (all of France vs. Paris only), (2) Policy shock (2015 REP reform as DiDisc, not just cross-sectional), (3) Mechanism decomposition (private school outside option as structural moderator), (4) Data universe (DVF 2014-2024, ~30M transactions vs. limited notarial records), (5) Built-in multi-boundary placebos (non-REP/non-REP boundaries).
- **Specific contribution delta (1-2 sentences):** We provide the first national-scale quasi-experimental test of whether government education quality labels causally move housing markets, exploiting France's 2015 REP reform as a difference-in-discontinuity shock at collège catchment boundaries. The private school interaction decomposes whether capitalization reflects assignment rigidity (carte scolaire) or pure neighborhood information, extending Fack & Grenet's cross-sectional Paris result to a causal national design.
