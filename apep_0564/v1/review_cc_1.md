# Internal Review Round 1

**Reviewer:** Claude Code (Self-Review)
**Verdict:** Major Revision (but publishable with honest framing)

## Summary

This paper attempts to use immigration judge leniency as an IV for asylum grant rates to study local labor market effects. The first stage is exceptionally strong (F=855), but the identification strategy fails its built-in placebo test: high-wage sectors respond as strongly as low-wage sectors. The paper honestly reports this failure and frames the contribution as methodological.

## Strengths

1. **Intellectual honesty:** The paper does not hide the placebo failure. It leads with the negative finding in the abstract and devotes a full subsection to diagnosing the problem. This is rare and commendable.

2. **Strong first stage:** F=855 is extraordinary. The raw material for a credible IV exists; the limitation is data access, not design.

3. **Clear path forward:** The discussion of what data and specification changes would achieve credible identification is concrete and actionable.

4. **Good institutional background:** The description of EOIR, judge assignment, and the legal status channel is thorough and accurate.

## Weaknesses

1. **No causal estimate survives.** The paper's central contribution is documenting a failure. While this is scientifically honest, it limits the paper's policy contribution.

2. **Cross-sectional instrument is fundamentally limited.** The discussion of *why* the design fails is clear, but more could be done to quantify *how much* confounding exists (e.g., partial R² of instrument after controls).

3. **Some figures reference issues.** The paper references figures in the appendix (fig1, fig2, etc.) that should be verified in the PDF.

4. **Balance test reporting.** The balance test section could more clearly present the joint F-test result.

## Recommendation

Proceed with external review. The paper is honest, well-written, and makes a genuine methodological contribution. The negative finding is the main result, and it is presented clearly.
