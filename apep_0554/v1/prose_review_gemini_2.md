- **Greatest strength:** **Economy of phrasing.** Phrases like "demographic oblivion," "check in the mail," and "dose-response" do heavy lifting with very few characters.

### Top 5 Concrete Improvements

1.  **Kill the Roadmap (Page 4):** Delete the "The remainder of the paper proceeds as follows..." paragraph. Your section headers and transitions are strong enough that the reader doesn't need a table of contents in prose.
2.  **Punch up the "Mechanism" topic sentence (Page 17):** Instead of *"Why would shorter hours fail to help fertility? The answer lies in the trade-off..."*, try: *"The reform freed time but at a price: the overtime pay that keeps Korean households solvent."*
3.  **Active Voice in the Data Section:** On Page 9, instead of *"The analysis draws on..."*, use *"I use three data sources..."* It’s more direct and aligns with the 80% active voice goal.
4.  **Eliminate "It is important to note" (Page 22):** You write, *"That the estimate remains large... is important."* Just say: *"The estimate remains large and highly significant even after conditioning on macro covariates, suggesting..."* Let the reader decide if it's important based on the fact.
5.  **Strengthen the final sentence (Page 27):** The current final sentence is good, but make it Shleifer-short. 
    *   *Current:* "freeing time does not help if the cost of children exceeds what reduced earnings can bear."
