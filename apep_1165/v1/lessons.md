## Discovery
- **Idea selected:** idea_0702 — Swiss municipal mergers and functional spending decomposition. Chose for novel angle (functional decomposition) vs. existing APEP papers on democratic costs.
- **Data source:** Canton Zurich Statistical Office (opendata.swiss) — functional spending per municipality since 1990. BFS Historisiertes Gemeindeverzeichnis for merger events.
- **Key risk:** Small treated sample (8 merger events, 18 dissolved municipalities in ZH). National data (EFV) turned out to be canton-level aggregates only.

## Execution
- **What worked:** The functional decomposition is the paper's strength. Clear separation between administration (significant decline) and nine service functions (precise nulls). The "overhead illusion" framing resonated.
- **What didn't:** National municipal-level functional spending data doesn't exist on BFS open data. The EFV Finanzstatistik is canton-level aggregates. Had to pivot from a national study to single-canton.
- **Data pivot:** Originally planned ~350 national merger events. Ended up with 8 ZH merger events because Zurich is the only canton publishing per-municipality functional spending on opendata.swiss.
