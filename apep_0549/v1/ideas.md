# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: Online Sports Betting and Alcohol-Involved Fatal Traffic Crashes
**Policy:** State legalization of online/mobile sports betting (26 states, staggered 2018-2024), triggered by the May 2018 PASPA ruling. Treatment dates: NJ Jun 2018, PA Jul 2019, IN Oct 2019, CO May 2020, IL Jun 2020, MI Jan 2021, AZ Sep 2021, NY Jan 2022, OH Jan 2023, MA Mar 2023, NC Mar 2024.
**Outcome:** NHTSA FARS microdata (1975-2022): universe of fatal motor vehicle crashes with exact date, hour, county, state, and person-level alcohol involvement. Primary: state-year alcohol-involved fatal crash rate. Secondary: alcohol crash share, nighttime (8PM-3AM) crashes, Sunday crashes.
**Identification:** Staggered DiD (Callaway-Sant'Anna 2021) with 26 treated states and ~24 controls. Three mechanism tests: (1) NFL game-day triple-diff (crash counts on NFL Sundays vs non-game Sundays × post-betting), (2) NFL-market counties vs non-NFL counties within treated states, (3) nighttime alcohol crashes vs daytime crashes. Placebo: non-alcohol fatal crashes (no mechanism linking betting to sober driving).
**Why it's novel:** Taylor et al. (2024) documented 20% alcohol spending increase from online betting but no health consequences measured. Hollenbeck (2025) studied financial distress. No paper connects the alcohol spillover from sports betting to fatal traffic crashes — a cross-domain mechanism (gambling → alcohol consumption → impaired driving deaths) with first-order welfare stakes (VSL × excess fatalities vs state gambling tax revenue).
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: 26 treated states staggered 2018-2024, ~24 controls. FARS freely downloadable from NHTSA FTP (34.7MB/year, all 50 states + DC). 39,422 fatal crashes/year (8,802 alcohol-involved in 2022). NFL game day amplification confirmed in raw data (Sunday alcohol share 31.5% vs Tuesday 15.8%).

## Idea 2: Follow the Money or Follow the Crime? Police Resource Allocation After Civil Asset Forfeiture Reform
**Policy:** 37 states + DC reformed civil asset forfeiture laws (2014-2024). Four intensity levels: abolition (NM 2015, NE 2016), conviction requirement (16 states), raised burden of proof (16 states), reporting only (27 states). 13 never-reformed states as controls.
**Outcome:** FBI UCR Arrests by Age, Sex, and Race (ICPSR 102263, 1974-2021). Agency-level annual arrest counts by offense type: drug arrests vs property crime arrests vs violent crime arrests. Secondary: FBI UCR Offenses Known and Clearances (ICPSR 100707).
**Identification:** Callaway-Sant'Anna staggered DiD with intensity coding (0-3 scale). Substitution test: does removing forfeiture revenue cause police to shift from drug enforcement (which generates seizures) toward property/violent crime enforcement? Heterogeneity by pre-reform police reliance on forfeiture revenue, reform intensity, state fiscal health.
**Why it's novel:** Existing research asks "does abolishing forfeiture increase crime?" (answer: no). No one has asked the substitution question: when forfeiture revenue disappears, do police reallocate effort across crime types? This is a revealed-preference test of police incentive responsiveness.
**Feasibility check:** 37 reformed states, 13 controls. UCR data publicly available on ICPSR. Institute for Justice reform database confirms treatment dates.

## Idea 3: When the Canal Runs Dry: Trade Costs and Port Diversion from the 2023-24 Panama Canal Drought
**Policy:** El Nino-driven drought forced 50% reduction in Panama Canal daily ship transits (36→18) from July 2023 to February 2024. Genuinely exogenous weather shock to transit capacity.
**Outcome:** Monthly port-level merchandise imports from US Census Bureau International Trade Data API. ~100 US customs ports, monthly, by country of origin and HS commodity code. Freely accessible.
**Identification:** Continuous treatment DiD: East Coast ports (30-50% Canal-dependent for Asian imports) vs West Coast ports (zero Canal dependence). Origin-country × port double-difference absorbs port-level demand and country-level supply shocks. Port diversion test: West Coast should see increased volumes during drought.
**Why it's novel:** No econometric paper estimates causal trade diversion effects of the Panama Canal drought. Feyrer (2021, REStud) studies 1967 Suez closure at country-pair level. Unique: genuinely exogenous, recent, granular monthly port data, clean East/West contrast.
**Feasibility check:** Census API confirmed working, ~35 major ports × 18 months. Savannah Chinese imports showed decline during drought peak.
