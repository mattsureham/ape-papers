## Discovery
- **Idea selected:** idea_0709 — NIS2 cybersecurity size threshold (50 employees), first causal study
- **Data source:** Eurostat isoc_cisce_ra (triennial ICT security survey) — clean API access, 33,845 rows, no issues
- **Key risk:** Only 2-3 pre-treatment periods (triennial survey), aggregate country × size-class data

## Execution
- **What worked:** The compliance theater decomposition (formal vs technical measures) produced a vivid, named finding. Compulsory training (+3.67 pp, p<0.001) as the strongest individual effect tells a clear story. The mandated vs non-mandated interaction (p=0.03) nailed the mechanism.
- **What didn't:** Aggregate index-level effects are imprecise — the story lives in the decomposition, not the headline DiD. The DDD with transposition timing was uninformative (null), likely because firms respond to the EU-level signal regardless of national transposition.
- **Review feedback adopted:** Added sector coverage limitation (NIS2 covers specific sectors but data is cross-sector aggregate), strengthened anticipation/timing discussion (2024 fieldwork precedes Oct deadline), added MDE calculation (1.8 pp at 80% power), added questionnaire stability note. Reviewers also raised ecological inference concern (country × size aggregate) — acknowledged but unavoidable with available data.
