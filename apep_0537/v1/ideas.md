# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: Generative AI as Seniority-Biased Technological Change: Evidence from SEC Filings and Occupational Employment Data
**Policy:** The diffusion of generative AI across U.S. firms beginning with ChatGPT's release in November 2022. By 2024, 770+ publicly traded firms disclosed generative AI adoption in 10-K filings (up from 2 in 2022). This creates a sharp, time-varying, industry-level treatment intensity that can be matched to occupation-level employment.
**Outcome:** BLS Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OEWS): annual occupation × industry × state employment counts covering 830+ occupations and all NAICS industries, 2015-2024. Combined with O*NET Job Zones (1-5 scale of experience/education required) to classify occupations as entry-level (Zones 1-2) vs. senior (Zones 4-5). Cross-validated with QCEW quarterly industry × county employment and wages.
**Identification:** Continuous-treatment DiD exploiting industry-level variation in GenAI adoption intensity (share of firms in 4-digit NAICS disclosing GenAI in 10-K filings) × occupation seniority (O*NET Job Zone). Triple-difference: industry × seniority × post-2023 interacted with Felten-Raj-Seamans AI Occupational Exposure scores. Pre-period: 2015-2022 (8 years). Post-period: 2023-2024 (2 years). Key placebo: occupations with high Job Zone but low AI exposure should show no differential effect.
**Why it's novel:** Hosseini Maasoum & Lichtinger (2025) documented seniority-biased technological change using proprietary résumé data. This paper tests the same hypothesis with entirely independent public data: administrative employer filings (SEC EDGAR 10-Ks) for the treatment and government survey data (BLS OEWS) for outcomes. The industry-level design complements their firm-level approach and allows occupation-level heterogeneity analysis impossible with résumé data.
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: EDGAR EFTS API returns GenAI mention counts by year (0→2→90→770→1266 from 2021-2025). BLS OEWS flat files downloadable for 2015-2024. O*NET Job Zone data freely available. QCEW CSV files available via API. Felten-Raj-Seamans AIOE scores on GitHub. All public, no authentication barriers except O*NET (API key available).

## Idea 2: Did AI-Exposed Industries Hollow Out Entry-Level Jobs? Evidence from QCEW Payroll Records
**Policy:** Same GenAI diffusion shock. Treatment intensity measured by industry-level AI occupational exposure (Felten-Raj-Seamans AIOE aggregated to NAICS) rather than firm-level 10-K disclosures.
**Outcome:** QCEW quarterly establishment-level payroll records: county × industry × quarter employment levels and average weekly wages. Higher-frequency (quarterly vs. annual) enables sharper event-study around ChatGPT release. Cross-reference with CPS microdata on worker age, tenure, and education within industry cells.
**Identification:** Continuous-treatment DiD: high-AIOE vs. low-AIOE industries, pre vs. post November 2022. Triple-diff adds young worker (age <30) vs. prime-age (30-54) from CPS within industry. Pre-trends from 2015-2022 quarterly data.
**Why it's novel:** Uses payroll-based administrative records (QCEW covers 95% of U.S. jobs) rather than job postings or résumés. Quarterly frequency enables sharper identification of the ChatGPT timing break. CPS microdata decomposition separates age/experience from occupation effects.
**Feasibility check:** QCEW API confirmed working, returns CSV by year/quarter/area. CPS-IPUMS available (though IPUMS key not configured — can use Census API for basic ASEC tabulations). AIOE scores freely downloadable.

## Idea 3: Earnings Call Signals and Junior Employment: Do Firms That Talk About AI Hire Fewer Beginners?
**Policy:** Same GenAI diffusion. Treatment measured via earnings call transcript mentions of GenAI-related terms, constructing firm × quarter adoption intensity from SEC 8-K and investor presentation filings.
**Outcome:** OEWS for occupation-level employment + QCEW for industry aggregates. Additional outcome: CPS displaced worker supplement and job tenure supplement for direct evidence on separations vs. hiring freezes at the occupation × industry level.
**Identification:** Shift-share / Bartik-style: firm-level GenAI talk intensity aggregated to industry-MSA cells, interacted with local occupation mix pre-ChatGPT. Compares MSAs with high vs. low initial employment share in GenAI-exposed occupations.
**Why it's novel:** Combines high-frequency firm-level signals (quarterly earnings calls) with geographic employment variation. Shift-share design addresses endogeneity of firm-level adoption decisions.
**Feasibility check:** 8-K filings searchable via EDGAR EFTS. However, earnings call transcripts require commercial data (Refinitiv, S&P Capital IQ) — may need to proxy with 8-K and 10-Q filings. Shift-share design adds complexity. Partially feasible.
