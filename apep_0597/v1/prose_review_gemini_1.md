# Section-by-Section Review

## The Opening
**Verdict:** [Hooks immediately]
The opening is masterful. You avoid the "economic throat-clearing" trap and instead place the reader in a specific moment: Eagle Square, May 29, 2023. By quoting the single sentence ("the fuel subsidy is gone") and immediately following it with a concrete price comparison between Lagos and Maiduguri, you make the geographic gradient visible before you even define it. It follows the Shleifer/Glaeser playbook perfectly: a vivid observation followed by the stakes.

## Introduction
**Verdict:** [Shleifer-ready]
This is exceptionally tight. You explain the "natural experiment" of turning on a cost gradient that was previously "invisible." The transition from the fuel price shock to the "pumps to plates" channel (Section 1, Para 4) is smooth and establishes the human stakes (Katz-style).
*   **Specific Suggestion:** In the results preview (Para 5), you use "log-point larger petrol price increase." For a Shleifer-standard paper, try to lead with the most intuitive unit. Instead of "0.9 log-point," consider: "each additional 100 kilometers from a terminal added roughly 1 percent to the price of fuel." Keep the technical coefficients for the tables.
