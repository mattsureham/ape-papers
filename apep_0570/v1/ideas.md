# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: The Anatomy of Tax Pass-Through: Malaysia's GST-to-SST Switch and Product-Level Price Adjustment
**Policy:** Malaysia's 2018 dual tax regime change: 6% GST zeroed June 1, 2018 (16 days after surprise election), then SST (5-10% sales tax, 6% service tax) reintroduced September 1, 2018. Creates three natural product groups: (A) standard-rated under GST AND covered by SST (full treatment — price drops then partial recovery), (B) standard-rated under GST but NOT covered by SST (permanent windfall — price drops and stays low), (C) zero-rated/exempt under GST (control — no price change in either period).
**Outcome:** OpenDOSM CPI at 4-digit class level (101 product classes, monthly, January 2010 - January 2026). 19,493 observations. Available as Parquet files at `https://storage.dosm.gov.my/cpi/cpi_4d.parquet`. Secondary: 2-digit division CPI (14 divisions, 2000-2026). Data access confirmed — no API key required.
**Identification:** Triple-difference (product tax status x pre/post-June x pre/post-September). First difference: before vs. after June 1 (GST zeroing). Second difference: standard-rated products (treated) vs. zero-rated/exempt (control). Third difference: among treated, SST-covered (partial recovery) vs. not SST-covered (permanent reduction). 8+ years pre-treatment data for parallel trends. The June 1 zeroing was sudden (announced May 16, implemented June 1). The election outcome was genuinely surprising (first government change since 1957). Product tax classifications predetermined by legislation, not chosen by firms. Two sequential shocks provide internal replication.
**Why it's novel:** No product-level DDD study of Malaysia's GST-SST switch exists. Existing literature (Leow & Yew 2019; Saari et al. 2023) uses input-output models or aggregate CPI. Sequential removal-then-reimposition of indirect taxes is essentially unique globally — enables direct comparison of upward vs. downward pass-through for the SAME products, providing a clean test of asymmetric price adjustment (Peltzman 2000 AER). Zero APEP overlap.
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: 101 product classes with heterogeneous GST/SST treatment; data shows clear differential price drops in June 2018 (clothing -2.6%, furnishings -2.4%, recreation -2.9% vs. housing -0.5%). Parquet files download without authentication. 70+ treated units, 30+ control classes. READY.
