# Literature Novelty Check

## Core literatures
1. **Bunching:** Saez (2010), Kleven & Waseem (2013), Garicano et al. (2016), Kleven (2016). No existing paper documents bunching at renewable energy capacity thresholds with the four-break design used here.
2. **Renewable energy policy:** Borenstein (2012), Hughes & Podolefsky (2015), Gillingham & Tsvetanov (2018), Crago & Chernyakhovskiy (2017). These study adoption responses to financial incentives but not bunching at capacity thresholds.
3. **Intermediated choice:** Chetty et al. (2011), Chetty (2009 salience). The installer channel in this paper connects to this literature by showing that intermediated choices can produce much larger responses than household-based settings.

## Novelty assessment
No published paper documents: (a) bunching at energy capacity thresholds, (b) a four-break event study at a single bunching threshold, or (c) the kink-notch decomposition at a single threshold with the same agents. The contribution is novel.
