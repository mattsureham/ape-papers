## Discovery
- **Idea selected:** idea_1535 — Section 301 tariffs × Asian-White wage gap via QWI race panel. Chosen for massive data (143.9M rows), novel racial angle, and clean tariff variation.
- **Data source:** QWI Race-Hispanic panel (Azure) — 12.9M rows loaded, but only 2-digit NAICS available for race data (3-digit/4-digit not available). This was the critical constraint.
- **Key risk:** Pre-trends and coarse sector-level variation

## Execution
- **What worked:** The composition windfall framing — naming the mechanism made the paper memorable. Black-White placebo yielding opposite sign was a strong supporting result.
- **What didn't:** QWI race data at 2-digit NAICS is too coarse for within-manufacturing variation. Pre-trends rejected. COVID-era amplification confounds the tariff channel.
- **Review feedback adopted:** Prioritized pre-COVID estimate as primary specification, added economic magnitude translation, addressed log vs level discrepancy, downgraded causal claims.
