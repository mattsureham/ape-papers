# Reply to Reviewers — apep_0869 v3

## Reviewer 1 (GPT-5.4 R1)

### Pre-trend / anticipation
> The identifying variation is not yet convincingly isolated from differential pre-trends.

We agree this is the paper's most important limitation. The v3 revision addresses it transparently: the trimmed-window estimate (-9.4%, Table 4) excludes the 2017-2018 period and remains significant. We present a range (-9.4% to -11.7%) rather than a single headline estimate, and the abstract and conclusion both acknowledge the fragility.

### Inference
> Inference is too weak to support the current level of confidence.

The v3 revision presents RI as the primary inferential frame throughout. The timing-permutation p=0.077 is described as "marginally significant at the 10% level," and the state-permutation p=0.167 is reported without claiming rejection. All conventional p<0.001 language has been removed from headlines.

### O*NET post-treatment
> The treatment intensity measure is partly post-treatment.

Section 5.2 now includes a detailed discussion of the post-treatment measurement concern, noting that O*NET measures structural technological requirements of occupations rather than firm-level adoption decisions responsive to state-specific litigation risk.

### Border vs aggregate
> The evidence is more consistent with border reallocation than broad incidence.

Agreed. The v3 revision explicitly discusses both interpretations and frames the border estimate as an upper bound.
