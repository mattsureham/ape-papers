# Reply to Reviewers

## Reviewer 1 (GPT-5.4 R1)

### Pre-trend / anticipation concern
> "A significant placebo in a relatively short pre-period is direct evidence against the maintained identifying assumption."

We agree this is the paper's most important limitation. We have made three changes:
1. The trimmed-window estimate (-9.4%, excluding 2017-2018) is now the **headline result** throughout the paper, with the full-sample -11.7% presented as an upper bound.
2. All causal language has been downshifted from "demonstrates" to "suggests" or "is consistent with."
3. The pre-trend discussion now explicitly describes the localized 2018 bump and notes that a linear trend control absorbs the result, presenting this as evidence for caution about magnitudes rather than dismissing it.

We cannot fully resolve the pre-trend concern in this cycle. The paper now transparently acknowledges this as a core limitation.

### Six-cluster inference
> "RI p=0.167 does not support conventional significance."

We have restructured the inference presentation: randomization inference (p=0.077 for timing, p=0.167 for state) is now the **primary inferential frame**. Clustered SEs are presented as descriptive benchmarks. Language like "p<0.001" no longer appears as the headline.

### O*NET post-treatment construction
> "2025 O*NET is post-treatment."

We have revised the text to acknowledge that the O*NET database version postdates the ruling, with a justification: occupational task content measures structural technological characteristics that change slowly and are determined by technology requirements, not state-level litigation regimes. We acknowledge this as a limitation.

### Control-group validity
> "Unclear whether Finance/Healthcare are truly exempt."

The continuous-exposure design addresses this directly: Finance and Healthcare receive intermediate scores (0.42 and 0.34 after preemption discounts), not zero. They serve as low-exposure benchmarks, not clean controls.
