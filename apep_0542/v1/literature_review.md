- **Selected idea title:** When the Train Doesn't Come: The Property Value Destruction of HS2's Northern Cancellation
- **Why this is not duplicative:** No existing academic paper estimates property value effects of the HS2 Phase 2 cancellation. The transit capitalization literature overwhelmingly studies new lines opening (positive shocks); infrastructure cancellation provides a rare negative shock that tests whether anticipatory capitalization was real by observing its destruction. The within-project control (Phase 1 still proceeding) is uniquely powerful.
- **Specific contribution delta (1-2 sentences):** We provide the first causal estimate of negative infrastructure capitalization from the surprise cancellation of a major rail project, using the universe of English property transactions and a within-project control group. We decompose the effect into station-access loss (negative) and corridor-blight relief (potentially positive), revealing the heterogeneous channels through which infrastructure expectations affect housing markets.
