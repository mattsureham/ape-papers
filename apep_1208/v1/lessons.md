## Discovery
- **Idea selected:** idea_1619 — Ghana's DDEP is a rare, clean natural experiment for domestic sovereign restructuring effects on credit
- **Data source:** World Bank WDI API — reliable, complete for 14/16 SSA countries (Ethiopia and Cameroon dropped for missing credit data)
- **Key risk:** Country-level SCM with N=1 treated unit; heavy reliance on Nigeria (94% weight) as primary donor

## Execution
- **What worked:** The SCM design clearly identifies the post-DDEP credit collapse (-7.18 pp). The NPL mechanism channel (+4.53 pp) is clean and significant. The portable mechanism ("credit desert") frames the contribution well.
- **What didn't:** DiD event study reveals pre-trends at t-3 and t-2 (COVID years 2020-2021), compromising the parallel trends assumption. Nigeria's dominance in SCM weights makes the comparison effectively Ghana-vs-Nigeria. Only 1 post-treatment year limits dynamic analysis.
- **Review feedback adopted:** Fixed magnitude arithmetic (49% → 64% relative to 2022 baseline); softened causal language ("suggestive causal evidence"); added bank-level data limitation caveat; added GDP denominator construct validity note; reframed effect as "acceleration" beyond pre-existing trend rather than clean level effect
