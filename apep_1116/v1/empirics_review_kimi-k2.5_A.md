### 1. Idea Fidelity

The paper pursues the core research agenda outlined in the manifest: exploiting quasi-random examiner reassignment within continuation patent families to measure the causal contribution of examiner identity to outcomes. The key design elements—using art-unit-by-year fixed effects, leave-one-out leniency instruments, and small-entity heterogeneity—are all present. However, the paper diverges materially from the manifest’s reported statistics without explanation. The manifest claims 135,624 same-AU reassigned twin pairs and a 36.0% raw discordance rate, while the paper reports 97,922 pairs (a 28% reduction) and 29.8% discordance (a 17% relative decrease). Similarly, the "excess discordance" shifts from 4.2pp in the manifest to 11.0pp (raw) or 7.7pp (regression-adjusted) in the paper. These discrepancies suggest either sample redefinition or coding errors that must be reconciled. The manifest also emphasized technology-center variation (organic chemistry 65% discordance vs. design 14%), which would strengthen the paper but is relegated to a passing mention here.
