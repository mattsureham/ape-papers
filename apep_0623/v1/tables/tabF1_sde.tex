{\footnotesize \emph{Notes:} This table reports standardized effect sizes (SDE) to facilitate cross-study comparison of treatment effect magnitudes. For binary (0/1) treatments, SDE $= \hat{\beta} / \text{SD}(Y)$ and the SD($X$) column is marked ``---''. For continuous treatments, SDE $= \hat{\beta} \times \text{SD}(X) / \text{SD}(Y)$, which gives the effect of a one-standard-deviation change in the treatment variable, measured in standard deviations of the outcome.

\textbf{Research question:} Does the TCJA \$10K SALT deduction cap reduce house prices in high-SALT zip codes, and does the 2025 OBBB reversal restore them?
