# Revision Plan (Stage C)

## Summary of Reviewer Feedback

All three reviewers praise the novel "reverse treatment" question and the honest treatment of limitations. The core concern is that the causal identification fails: pre-trends reject parallel trends, IPW eliminates the effect, and Rambachan-Roth bounds include zero.

## Planned Changes

### 1. Reframe narrative explicitly as descriptive (All reviewers)
- Remove any remaining causal language ("caused," "reduced," "effect of losing status")
- Frame as documenting the "association between status loss and economic trajectories"
- Lead with the selection story rather than burying it in Discussion

### 2. Address IPW result more thoroughly (Gemini)
- Explain why IPW eliminates the effect: it accounts for the pre-trend differential
- Note that the IPW result confirms selection drives the raw DiD
- Frame the contribution as: "the correlation is real and policy-relevant even if not purely causal"

### 3. Strengthen the "what we learn despite weak causality" argument
- Even if selection explains the correlation, the descriptive finding matters for policy
- Phase-out design implications don't require causality — they follow from the correlation pattern
- The sharp break at 2015 is still informative about the timing of divergence

### 4. Fix remaining minor issues
- Ensure "quasi-random" language is removed or qualified
- Verify all claims match reported results
