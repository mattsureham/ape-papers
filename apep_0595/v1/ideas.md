# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: Trade Protection by Fiat: The Price Effects of Nigeria's 2019 Land Border Closure
**Policy:** Nigeria's sudden 15-month land border closure (August 2019 - December 2020). President Buhari ordered closure of all land borders to goods movement on August 20, 2019, with no advance announcement. Borders with Benin, Niger, Cameroon, and Chad were sealed to commercial goods. The closure targeted rice smuggling and aimed to promote domestic production. Partial reopening in December 2020, full reopening early 2021.
**Outcome:** WFP Global Food Prices Database via Humanitarian Data Exchange (HDX). Nigeria dataset: 56,163 observations across 68 markets, 14 states, 43 commodities (2002-2026). Rice: 34 markets with 24+ monthly observations in 2017-2021 window. Markets have latitude/longitude coordinates for distance-to-border calculations. Cross-border data: Benin (50 rice markets, 2,737 obs 2017-2021) and Niger (extensive market network from 1990). Primary outcomes: log(rice price/kg), log(maize price/kg), log(sorghum price/kg) in local currency and USD.
**Identification:** Spatial difference-in-differences. Treatment intensity = distance from each market to nearest international land border. Border markets (<150km) are treated; interior markets (>300km) are controls. Identifying assumption: absent closure, border and interior prices would follow parallel trends (testable with 31 pre-period months). Sudden, nationwide policy eliminates selection concerns. Robustness: Callaway & Sant'Anna (2021), placebo on non-tradeable commodities, synthetic control on individual markets, cross-border validation (Benin prices should fall).
**Why it's novel:** No published paper uses WFP market-level geo-referenced monthly price data with spatial DiD to estimate the causal price effect of the border closure. Cross-border spillover estimation (Benin/Niger mirror image) is entirely novel. Does not overlap with APEP 0463 (currency redesign).
**Feasibility check:** Variation confirmed (34 markets spanning border to deep interior, 39-515km from border). Data access confirmed (HDX CSV, no auth). Novelty confirmed (no top-journal publication with spatial DiD on this policy). Sample size: ~34 markets x 60 months x 3 rice types = ~6,120 market-month-commodity observations.
