## Discovery
- **Idea selected:** idea_2546 — X-waiver elimination and buprenorphine desert entry. Chose for sharp federal policy shock + confirmed T-MSIS data + mechanism surprise framing.
- **Data source:** T-MSIS Medicaid Provider Spending (Azure) + NPPES + HRSA HPSA — all worked smoothly, no API issues.
- **Key risk:** J-codes capture only injectable buprenorphine, not sublingual prescriptions. All three reviewers flagged this as the main limitation.

## Execution
- **What worked:** The "credential gap fallacy" framing — naming the mechanism upfront — creates a sticky, tournament-competitive result. 85.7% concentration in served counties is a striking number. Permutation inference p < 0.002 is convincing.
- **What didn't:** The DiD is somewhat mechanical (pre-treatment outcomes are zero in deserts by construction). A descriptive decomposition with the DiD as supporting evidence may have been a stronger framing than leading with the regression.
- **Review feedback adopted:** Added explicit J-code limitation throughout, qualified desert counts, strengthened pre-trend and telehealth discussions, scaled back Discussion rhetoric. GPT-5.4 suggested reframing around descriptive decomposition — a V2 could reorganize around cross-sectional destination shares with DiD supporting.
