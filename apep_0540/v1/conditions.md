### Condition 4: making the paper explicitly about anticipation/construction timing rather than a generic transit ATE

**Status:** [x] RESOLVED

**Response:** The paper's central question is explicitly about the TIMING of capitalization: "When do property markets capitalize transit infrastructure — at announcement, during construction, or only at opening?" This is not "does transit raise property values" (known) but "when and how does the price response unfold under a decade-long infrastructure gestation?" The 2014-2025 DVF+ panel, combined with the multi-milestone timeline, enables decomposition of the total capitalization into three phases. The built-in placebo from delayed lines (opening 2028+) tests whether construction-phase effects exist independently of imminent service.

**Evidence:** Framing confirmed in research plan. McMillen & McDonald (2004) pioneered this for Chicago but with far smaller data; no comparable study exists for GPE at this scale.
