 **Referee Report: The Chilling Effect: How E-Verify Mandates Reduce Hispanic Employment Across State Lines**

---

### 1. Idea Fidelity

The paper pursues the original idea with high fidelity but one important substantive deviation. The manifest proposed a spatial triple-difference (DDD) design using Quarterly Workforce Indicators (QWI) to test whether Hispanic workers displaced by E-Verify mandates relocate to adjacent, never-treated counties (with a null result implying formality-informality substitution). The paper correctly implements the spatial DDD—exploiting variation in Hispanicvs.-Non-Hispanic employment, border versus interior counties in non-mandating states, and pre-post timing of adjacent state adoptions—and uses the workplace-based QWI data as specified. 

However, the empirical finding (a negative "chilling" effect rather than the hypothesized displacement or null) is treated as the definitive result rather than one possibility requiring additional validation. The paper departs from the manifest’s emphasis on ruling out geographic displacement as the primary explanation; instead, it interprets the negative coefficient as employer-driven chilling without fully ruling out worker-side responses (e.g., out-migration to distant informal labor markets) that would also generate negative border-county employment in QWI data. This interpretive asymmetry is not fatal but requires additional robustness checks to support the strong mechanism claims made in the abstract and introduction.

---

### 2. Summary

This paper uses staggered state-level adoptions of E-Verify mandates (2008–2023) to estimate geographic spillovers on Hispanic employment in adjacent, non-mandating states. Using county-quarter-ethnicity data from the QWI, the author implements a triple-difference design comparing Hispanic versus Non-Hispanic employment in border versus interior counties before and after neighboring states adopt mandates. The central finding is a “chilling effect”: Hispanic employment falls by roughly 16 log points in border counties relative to interior controls, concentrated in accommodation and food services and operating through reduced hiring rather than layoffs.
