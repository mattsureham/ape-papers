# Internal Review — Round 1

**Paper:** Do Administrative Borders Tax Electricity? A Multi-Border Spatial RDD of Swiss Cantonal Energy Policy
**Verdict:** Minor Revision

## Summary

The paper asks whether cantonal energy law reforms create meaningful electricity price discontinuities at Swiss cantonal borders. Using 50,509 municipality-year observations from ElCom and a spatial RDD design with 22 mixed border pairs, it finds cantonal charges explain only 2% of total price variance. The built-in federal aid fee placebo validates the design convincingly.

## Strengths

1. **Built-in placebo is excellent.** The federal aid fee (nationally uniform) provides an "opponent-killing" falsification test baked into the data structure. The near-zero coefficient (-0.002, SE=0.002) is very convincing.

2. **Novel tariff decomposition approach.** Component-by-component analysis provides mechanism identification rare in spatial RDD studies.

3. **Rich data.** 50,509 observations, 16 years, 22 mixed border pairs, 5 tariff components.

4. **Clean findings with policy relevance.** The 2% variance share is a precise, powerful null with clear policy implications for the Swiss Energiestrategie 2050 debate.

## Issues Requiring Revision

1. **Median discrepancy (FIXED).** Text stated median = 21.6 but Figure 1 showed 27.7. Corrected to match figure.

2. **Table numbering.** Verify all cross-references resolve correctly after label fixes.

3. **Limited literature engagement.** Only 11 citations in total. Could strengthen positioning relative to the broader regulated pricing and fiscal federalism literatures.

4. **Standard errors concern.** With only 8 reform cantons (26 total), canton-clustered SEs may understate true uncertainty. The paper acknowledges this but could discuss wild bootstrap or other small-cluster corrections.

5. **Page 10 white space.** Large gap between descriptive text and Figure 1 due to float placement. Consider adjusting figure placement.

## Verdict

The paper has a strong design, real data, clean results, and a meaningful policy contribution. The precise null finding is genuinely informative. Minor revisions to fix cross-references and strengthen the discussion of clustering are sufficient. **Minor Revision.**
