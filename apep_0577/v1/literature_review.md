# Early Literature Review — apep_0577

## Idea: No Registration, No Market (REACH 2018)

### Related Human Papers

1. **Becker and Henderson (2000)** — "Effects of Air Quality Regulations on Polluting Industries." JPE. Studies US Clean Air Act effects on plant births/deaths. Different regulation (environmental standards vs product registration), different outcome margin (plant location vs firm employment).

2. **Greenstone (2002)** — "The Impacts of Environmental Regulations on Industrial Activity." AER. Foundational paper on regulatory effects on manufacturing. US context, county-level analysis. No overlap with EU REACH or chemical registration.

3. **Walker (2013)** — "The Transitional Costs of Sectoral Reallocation." QJE. Studies Clean Air Act worker displacement. Methodologically related (environmental regulation → employment effects) but US context and different regulation type.

4. **European Commission (2018)** — "REACH Registration 2018 — 7 Years Later." Report. Survey-based, no econometric identification. Our paper provides the first causal evidence to complement this descriptive work.

5. **Milieu Ltd and Risk and Policy Analysts Ltd (2015)** — "REACH 2013 — Monitoring the Impacts." Commission report. Survey of 2013 deadline registrants. No causal identification.

### Overlap Risk: **LOW**

No published quasi-experimental study of REACH's industry effects exists. The Commission's own evaluations rely on surveys. The closest academic work (Becker-Henderson, Greenstone, Walker) studies US environmental regulations using entirely different identification strategies.

### Delta Statement

This paper provides the **first econometric estimate** of REACH's causal effects on chemical industry structure. It advances the literature in three ways: (1) exploiting the tonnage-phased design of REACH as a natural experiment, (2) identifying employment rather than entry/exit as the operative margin, and (3) using cross-country variation in firm-size composition as a continuous treatment intensity measure in a triple-difference framework.
