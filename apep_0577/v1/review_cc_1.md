# Internal Review - Round 1

## PART 1: CRITICAL REVIEW

### 1. Identification and Empirical Design

The triple-difference design exploiting time × sector × country-level micro-firm intensity is creative and well-motivated by the institutional features of REACH. The tonnage-phased structure provides a natural placebo (2013 deadline targeting large firms). The use of pre-2013 micro-firm shares for the placebo avoids post-treatment contamination.

Key concern: the pre-treatment convergence pattern in employment is non-trivial. The paper handles this honestly by noting it biases against finding negative effects, but a more formal trend-break test or parametric detrending would strengthen the claim.

### 2. Inference and Statistical Validity

Standard errors clustered at the country level (27 clusters) are adequate. RI yields p=0.064 for employment (marginally significant), consistent with the cluster-robust p=0.014. WCB failed to run — this should be noted as a limitation or debugged. LOO analysis is thorough for both outcomes.

### 3. Robustness and Alternative Explanations

Full robustness suite now covers both enterprise and employment outcomes. The Croatia exclusion test is valuable. Alt controls and alt timing are reassuring. The 2013 placebo is clean for both outcomes.

### 4. Contribution and Literature

Clear gap-filling: first causal evidence on REACH industry effects. Literature positioning against Greenstone (2002), Walker (2013), Becker-Henderson (2000), and Ryan (2012) is well-done. Could cite Dechezleprêtre and Sato (2017) environmental regulation survey.

### 5. Results Interpretation

The "downsizing not exit" framing is compelling and well-supported. Size-class heterogeneity (medium firms, not micro) is properly framed as theory-driven. Magnitude interpretation (6% per SD, 9.5% per IQR) is correct.

### 6. Actionable Revision Requests

1. **Must-fix:** Debug WCB or explain why it failed
2. **High-value:** Add trend-break test for employment convergence
3. **Optional:** Cite Dechezleprêtre and Sato (2017) survey of environmental regulation and competitiveness

### 7. Overall Assessment

Strong paper with a clean institutional setting, creative identification, and an interesting divergence between employment and enterprise effects. The pre-trend convergence is the main vulnerability but is handled with appropriate transparency.

DECISION: MINOR REVISION
