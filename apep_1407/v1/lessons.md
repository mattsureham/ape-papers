## Discovery
- **Idea selected:** idea_2455 — FEMA RR2.0 grandfathered premium shock, strong AGARA identification
- **Data source:** OpenFEMA FimaNfipPolicies — API painfully slow (1000 records/request, frequent 503s), checkpointing essential
- **Key risk:** Whether grandfathered vs non-grandfathered comparison captures the cap mechanism or just selection on commitment

## Execution
- **What worked:** The counterintuitive finding (cap trap > insurance denominator) makes the paper more interesting than the original hypothesis. Administrative data with clear treatment/control codes.
- **What didn't:** API rate limiting forced reduction from 10 to 5 states. NY data incomplete due to 503 errors. The 18% cap applies to ALL renewals (not just grandfathered) — three reviewers caught this institutional detail, requiring reframing.
- **Review feedback adopted:** Updated mechanism description to correctly reflect universal cap for renewals; added decomposition paragraph; fixed figure captions; expanded threats to validity with SUTVA, sample representativeness, and lapse measurement discussions.
