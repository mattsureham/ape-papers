## Discovery
- **Idea selected:** idea_2167 — UK Breathing Space moratorium (May 2021), first causal evaluation, clean jurisdictional boundary
- **Data source:** Insolvency Service annual location data (2015-2025), NOMIS population + claimant count, AiB Scotland national totals
- **Key risk:** Scotland LA-level data unavailable; pre-treatment intensity as dose prone to mean reversion

## Execution
- **What worked:** Data from Insolvency Service was excellent — 303 LAs, 11 years, complete type decomposition. The composition shift finding (bankruptcy → IVA substitution) is robust and novel. The Scotland comparison, though aggregate, adds valuable context.
- **What didn't:** Scotland LA-level data was inaccessible (AiB website restructured, statistics.gov.scot SPARQL down). Pre-trends in dose-response design were messy — significant placebo at 2018 limits causal interpretation. Would need border-level DiD with Scottish LAs for cleaner identification.
- **Review feedback adopted:** Toned down causal language throughout; added magnitude interpretation paragraph; added caveat paragraph about dose-response limitations; softened placebo discussion to acknowledge what design can and cannot identify.
