# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: Denmark's 2013 Disability Pension Gateway: Did Closing the Door to Disability Open the Door to Employment?
**Policy:** Denmark's 2013 Reform of Disability Pension and Flexjob Schemes (Lov nr. 1380 af 23/12/2012, effective January 1, 2013). Three simultaneous changes: (1) Under-40 disability pension ban — persons under 40 can no longer be awarded disability pension unless "evident" that work ability cannot be improved; (2) Resource scheme (ressourceforløb) — mandatory interdisciplinary vocational rehabilitation lasting up to 5 years before any disability pension award; (3) Flexjob expansion — subsidies proportional to actual hours worked, making mini-flexjobs (5-10 hours/week) viable.
**Outcome:** Statistics Denmark StatBank API (https://api.statbank.dk/v1/). Tables: AUK01 (public benefits by municipality, benefit type, sex, age, quarter, 2007Q1-2025Q3), RAS301 (employed persons by municipality, industry, socioeconomic status, age), INDKP111 (personal income by municipality, sex, age, income type, 1992-2024). No API key required.
**Identification:** Triple-difference design: Age group (treatment intensity) × Municipality (baseline disability prevalence) × Time (pre/post 2013Q1). High intensity: ages 25-39 (virtually barred). Moderate: ages 40-49 (resource scheme delay). Low: ages 50-59 (control-like). Pre-trends testable with 2007-2012 data. 98 municipalities × 6 age groups × 24 quarters pre-period.
**Why it's novel:** No existing age-group DiD or triple-diff on this reform. Existing studies use pre-post cohort designs (Madsen et al. 2021) or study different policies (Datta Gupta & Larsen 2015 on pre-2013 flexjob reimbursement). The substitution question — did closing the disability door genuinely push people into employment, or relabel them? — is unanswered. No APEP papers on Denmark, disability policy, or flexjobs.
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: Under-40 disability pension stock declined 33% (2013-2019) vs. 10% for 50-54; flex jobs grew 88% nationally; 98 municipalities with substantial heterogeneity. StatBank API returns municipality × benefit type × age × sex × quarter data with no authentication. ~42,336 cells; working-age population ~3.2 million.
