**1. Treatment mismeasurement (TH rate ≠ effective relief)**
We acknowledge this limitation. The pre-reform TH rate captures the *size* of the tax being abolished, not the effective relief to each household (which depends on income eligibility). However, the rate is the relevant margin for capitalization: property markets price the *tax on the property*, not the household-specific relief. We discuss this in Section 5.4 (identification).

**2. Sample composition break in 2021**
Addressed. We now report a balanced-panel specification (Table 5, Column 6) restricting to 2,214 communes observed in both pre- and post-reform periods. The coefficient is identical to the baseline (β = 0.0014, SE = 0.0007), confirming the result is not driven by compositional shifts.

**3. Department-by-year FE kills the estimate**
Acknowledged transparently in the revised robustness section. We discuss that the identifying variation is partly cross-department and that absorbing department-level trends eliminates the effect. This is now an explicit caveat.
