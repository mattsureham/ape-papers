## Discovery
- **Policy chosen:** Taxe d'habitation elimination (2018-2023) — €26B/year reform with cross-commune rate variation for continuous-treatment DiD. Merged with fiscal displacement (Idea 5) per tri-model panel consensus.
- **Ideas rejected:** Action Coeur de Ville (selection on decline, diffuse treatment), Denormandie (COVID confound), ZRR reform (saturated topic, EPCI clustering). Permis de louer was novel but no centralized adoption-date data.
- **Data source:** DVF (cadastre.data.gouv.fr 2014-2019, data.gouv.fr 2020-2025) + REI commune tax rates (data.economie.gouv.fr). Both confirmed publicly accessible.
- **Key risk:** Anticipation effects around 2017 Macron campaign could contaminate pre-trends. COVID-19 housing boom confounds post-2020.

## Review
- **Advisor verdict:** 3 of 4 PASS (GPT, Grok, Codex pass; Gemini fail)
- **Top criticism:** Net incidence decomposition (Part C) relied on a wrong-signed γ coefficient for TF→prices, making quantitative claims unreliable. Required complete reframing as "illustrative only" and removal of all quantitative net incidence claims from abstract/intro/conclusion.
- **Surprise feedback:** Outcome definition inconsistency (all property types pre-2021 vs apartment-only post-2021) was caught in advisor review — fixing to apartment-only throughout shifted β from near-zero to +0.0014. A seemingly minor data cleaning choice drove the entire result.
- **What changed:** (1) Apartment-only outcome throughout, (2) Net incidence reframed as illustrative with Figure 6 moved to appendix, (3) Balanced panel robustness check added (identical result), (4) Dept×year FE sensitivity discussed transparently as caveat, (5) Four decimal places in all tables.

## Summary
- **Title:** Who Bears the Tax Cut? Capitalization and Fiscal Displacement from France's Abolition of the Taxe d'Habitation
- **Method:** Continuous-treatment DiD (commune-level pre-reform TH rate × post-reform indicator)
- **Key finding:** β = 0.0014 (p=0.056) — modest but positive capitalization; fiscal displacement coefficient = 0.646 (p<0.01)
- **Biggest lesson:** Data harmonization across structural breaks in administrative data can completely change results. Always verify outcome definitions are consistent across time periods.
- **Process note:** Tables generated by modelsummary use tabularray format; manual conversion to booktabs required after each 06_tables.R run.
