# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: Winning the Peace: WWII Enlistment, GI Bill College Access, and Individual Occupational Upgrading
**Policy:** The Selective Training and Service Act (1940) initiated the first peacetime draft; after Pearl Harbor (December 1941), enlistment and conscription surged. State-level enlistment rates varied due to age composition, industrial deferment patterns, and agricultural deferment rules (Tydings Amendment). The Servicemen's Readjustment Act of 1944 (GI Bill) provided veterans college tuition, living stipends, and home loans. State enlistment rates per 1,000 men aged 18-35 (Selective Service System Special Monograph No. 10, 1956, digitized by NBER) range ~490-700 per 1,000 across states.
**Outcome:** incwage_1950, educ_1950, occscore_1950 from the MLP three-decade linked panel (1930-1940-1950). Panel adds incwage_1940 and educ_1940 as pre-treatment baselines; occscore_1930 as second pre-period. Secondary: ownershp_1950, ind1950_1950.
**Identification:** Two-stage IV. First stage: state enlistment rate (men 18-35, 1941-1944) instruments individual enlistment, interacted with birth cohort (1905-1922 = maximally draft-exposed). Second stage: post-war outcomes 1950. Pre-trend test: regress occscore_1940 - occscore_1930 on state enlistment rate for cohorts too old to serve (45-65 in 1940) as placebo. Robustness: exclude defense industry workers; control for state war-contract dollars per capita.
**Why it's novel:** Angrist 1990 (Vietnam), Bound and Turner 2002 (GI Bill aggregate) are the benchmarks. No paper uses the full 175M-record MLP crosswalk with a 1930 pre-baseline to estimate individual-level returns to WWII military service. The three-decade panel enables pre-trend testing impossible with two-decade designs.
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: 41.5M total 3-decade records; 9.1M draft-age men; occscore rises 20.43 to 24.05; incwage_1940, educ_1940, incwage_1950, educ_1950 all present. 48 state clusters. Selective Service enlistment data publicly available.
