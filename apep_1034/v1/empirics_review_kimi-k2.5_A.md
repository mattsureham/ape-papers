### 1. Idea Fidelity

The paper pursues the core research question outlined in the manifest—quantifying investment chilling from Norway’s December 2022 wind resource rent tax announcement—but implements only a subset of the proposed empirical strategies. Specifically, it executes the sector-level difference-in-differences (Strategy 2) using aggregate monthly production data, while abandoning the project-level event study (Strategy 1) and firm-level DiD (Strategy 3) that were central to the original identification plan. The cross-country validation using Eurostat (Norway vs. Sweden/Denmark) is included, but the planned analysis of petroleum investment trends as a concurrent shock to validate the control group is absent. Most critically, the paper shifts the empirical outcome from "investment" (a flow of new projects) to "production" (a stock of generated electricity), creating a significant tension between the research question and the measured outcome.
