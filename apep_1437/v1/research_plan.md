# Research Plan: Selective Atrophy — DOGE Hiring Freeze

## Question
How did the January 20, 2025 federal hiring freeze (and DOGE workforce contraction) reshape the *composition* of federal vacancies — across departments (military vs civilian), occupational families, GS grades, and locations?

## Identification
1. **Time-series event study** around Jan 20, 2025 EO using 2017-2025 USAJOBS Historic JOA monthly panel.
2. **Difference-in-differences:** military departments (DoD/Army/Navy/AF, exempt) vs civilian departments (treated). Two-way FE: department × month and calendar-month-of-year FE.
3. **Composition shift tests:** share of vacancies by occupation family (1800 oversight, 0400/1300 STEM, 0600 medical, 0300 admin) compared pre vs post.

## Data
- USAJOBS Historic JOA REST API (`data.usajobs.gov/api/historicjoa`), no auth required, monthly window queries.
- Period: Jan 2021 – Mar 2025 (51 months: 48 pre + 3 post).
- Aggregate to department × month panel; secondary: department × occ-family × month.

## Exposure Alignment
The treatment is Executive Order 14148 (Jan 20, 2025), which legally binds civilian executive-branch hiring authorities (HR offices and CHCOs at the 12 civilian cabinet departments and major independent agencies). The exposed unit is the federal hiring authority within each civilian department; the outcome (new external vacancy announcements) is generated by exactly that hiring authority via USAJOBS posting permissions. Military departments (Army, Navy, Air Force, DoD-wide) are statutorily exempted from EO 14148 and serve as the control. Sub-agency variation in actual exemptions ("public safety" carve-outs) is acknowledged as within-civilian heterogeneity rather than treatment misalignment. Workers who would have been hired absent the freeze are not directly observed; we measure the upstream margin (vacancy posting flow) which is the channel the EO directly regulates.

## Estimator
- Two-way FE OLS with department + month FE; CR1 SEs clustered at department.
- Outcome: log(1 + vacancies). Treated = civilian (military exempt). Post = >= 2025-02 (first full month post-freeze).
- Robustness: levels, Poisson FE, alternative cutoffs, drop military Jan 2025 partial month.

## SDE
Compute SDE for log(vacancies) treatment effect using SD of pooled outcome.

## Tables (≤5)
1. Summary statistics (departments, vacancy counts pre/post)
2. Main DiD: log vacancies civilian vs military
3. Event-study coefficients
4. Composition shifts by occupation family
5. Heterogeneity by department (top 8 civilian)
+ SDE appendix table.
