# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: Networked Anxiety Without Contact: Asylum Dispersal and SCI-Amplified RN Support
**Policy:** France's Schema National d'Accueil 2021-2023 (December 2020) mandated dispersal of asylum seekers from Ile-de-France (43-46% of registrations) to other regions. May 2022 decree capped IDF share at 23%. 16,000+ asylum seekers redirected. 3,400 new CADA + 1,500 CAES places created outside IDF.
**Outcome:** RN (Rassemblement National) vote share at department level from data.gouv.fr Parquet (107 departments, 11 elections 1999-2024). SCI NUTS3 from Humanitarian Data Exchange: 101 French departments, 10,201 pairs, CV=2.41.
**Identification:** Shift-share DiD. Shares: SCI(i,j) network weights (fixed, Facebook 2026). Shifts: department j's new asylum reception capacity. NetworkDispersal_i = SCI-weighted sum of new places. Prediction: network channel operates OPPOSITE to local contact effect. AKM standard errors for shift-share.
**Why it's novel:** Schneider-Strawczynski (2024) establishes local contact effect (hosting reduces RN). No paper tests network SPILLOVER. Inverts Allport: contact reduces prejudice locally, but network transmission AMPLIFIES it at distance. Mirror image of apep_0464 but with immigration shock.
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed — SCI NUTS3 CV=2.41; dispersal creates treatment across ~30-40 receiving departments; election data covers 1999-2024; sample ~96 metro depts × 4 elections = 384 obs. Data publicly accessible.
