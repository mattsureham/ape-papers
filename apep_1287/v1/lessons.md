## Discovery
- **Idea selected:** idea_1427 — 1927 Mississippi Flood as IV for Black farm worker displacement
- **Data source:** IPUMS MLP 1920-1930-1940 panel (Azure), flood instrument from Mississippi Alluvial Plain geography
- **Key risk:** Binary instrument (Alluvial Plain yes/no) rather than continuous inundation shares from Hornbeck & Naidu

## Execution
- **What worked:** The race-specific falsification is the paper's strongest card — null first stage for white workers in the same counties perfectly confirms the sharecropping trap mechanism. The MLP data from Azure was accessible and well-structured. The "sharecropping escape" framing gives the paper a vivid, portable mechanism.
- **What didn't:** Could not access the Hornbeck & Naidu replication data for continuous flood intensity, requiring the simpler binary instrument. The first-stage F (12.8 clustered) is borderline. The MLP 3-census linkage reduces the sample from ~167K to ~44K, introducing linkage selection concerns.
- **Review feedback adopted:** Added explicit discussion of exclusion restriction concerns (farm exit >1 coefficient reflects both migration and local economic destruction); reconciled F-stat discrepancy between text (12.8 clustered) and table (502.7 unclustered); acknowledged binary vs continuous instrument limitation; expanded linkage selection discussion.
