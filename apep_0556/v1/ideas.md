# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: Community Health Workers at Scale: India's ASHA Program and the Neonatal Mortality Transition
**Policy:** India's National Rural Health Mission (NRHM), launched April 2005, deployed ~900,000 Accredited Social Health Activists (ASHAs) — the world's largest community health worker program. Phase 1 (2005-06): 18 high-focus states received priority deployment with higher Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY) incentives (INR 1,400/institutional delivery). Phase 2 (2008-10): remaining states received ASHAs with lower JSY incentives (INR 800, restricted to BPL women). Within high-focus states, 264 districts received additional resources based on worst health parameters.
**Outcome:** DHS/NFHS subnational health indicators: neonatal mortality rate (NMR), infant mortality rate (IMR), institutional delivery rate, antenatal care (ANC 4+ visits), immunization coverage. Sample Registration System (SRS) provides annual state-level IMR/NMR time series (2000-2020) for high-frequency event studies. Supplemented by HMIS district-level panel (2008-2022) if accessible.
**Identification:** Generalized DiD comparing health outcome trajectories across 18 high-focus states (early ASHA, high JSY) vs 10+ non-high-focus states (late ASHA, low JSY). Triple-difference exploiting 264 high-focus districts within high-focus states. DHS birth history event study reconstructing annual cohort-specific mortality from NFHS-3/4/5 micro-data. Callaway-Sant'Anna heterogeneity-robust estimator. Built-in placebos: adult mortality, non-communicable disease outcomes unaffected by ASHAs; urban areas should show no ASHA effect. Mechanism decomposition: ASHA → institutional delivery → ANC → neonatal survival.
**Why it's novel:** No modern DiD with heterogeneity-robust estimators exists. Lim et al. (2010, Lancet) used propensity score matching; Powell-Jackson et al. (2015) found null effects using simple DiD with only 2 cross-sections. Neither exploits the HMIS district panel or DHS birth histories for annual event studies. The mechanism decomposition — tracing ASHA → facility delivery → neonatal survival — is absent from the literature.
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: 18+10 state groups with staggered timing, DHS API returns subnational NMR/IMR/institutional delivery for 5 survey rounds, SRS provides state-level annual mortality 2000-2020. Zero APEP overlap — no paper on NRHM/ASHA/neonatal mortality.

## Idea 2: Does Public Health Insurance Reduce Financial Vulnerability? Evidence from India's Ayushman Bharat Using NFHS Fieldwork Timing
**Policy:** Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY (launched September 2018) provides free health insurance up to Rs 5 lakh/family/year to bottom 40% of households via SECC 2011. ~30 states adopted; West Bengal, Delhi, and Odisha held out (own schemes). NFHS-5 fieldwork timing (Phase I: June 2019 vs Phase II: April 2021) creates exogenous variation in PM-JAY exposure duration (6-31 months).
**Outcome:** DHS API: health insurance coverage, institutional delivery, child anthropometry (stunting, wasting, underweight). ~40 subnational units × 2-3 survey rounds.
**Identification:** Holdout DiD (adopting vs 3 holdout states). Continuous exposure DiD using NFHS-5 phase assignment as exogenous exposure duration. Triple-difference: adoption × exposure × baseline uninsured rate.
**Why it's novel:** No rigorous multi-state DiD exists for PM-JAY. Fieldwork timing exploitation is original. Zero APEP overlap.
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: DHS API returns 324 records; 3 holdout states validated. Concern: thin N (~80 state-survey observations); systematic holdout selection.

## Idea 3: Banking the Unbanked Village: How Bank Branch Arrival Reshaped India's Rural Enterprise Landscape, 1990-2013
**Policy:** Three policy regimes created bank branch waves: post-liberalization contraction (1991-2004); 2005 Branch Authorization Policy (urban branches required corresponding underbanked-district branches); 2011 mandate (25% of new branches in unbanked rural centers). Treatment: village transition from zero to one bank branch.
**Outcome:** SHRUG Economic Census (ec90/98/05/13): total enterprises, employment, manufacturing/services split, female-owned firms, hired vs family labor. Nightlights (1992-2013). RBI bank branches with exact opening dates geocoded to shrug_id.
**Identification:** Event-study DiD around first bank branch arrival. Callaway-Sant'Anna with cohorts defined by EC period. Never-banked villages as comparison. IV using 2005 Branch Authorization Policy for rural placement. Spatial spillover test.
**Why it's novel:** Transforms Burgess & Pande (AER 2005) from 16 states to 500K villages. No paper exploits full 4-wave EC panel with geocoded branch opening dates for village-level effects.
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: all SHRUG tables free, 500K villages × 4 waves = 2M observations. Limitation: requires SHRUG download (~2-5GB), not currently on disk.
