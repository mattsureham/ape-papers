# Reply to Reviewers

## Reviewer 1 (GPT-5.4 R1): MAJOR REVISION

### 1. "Headline/newborn-survival claim exceeds evidence"
**Concern:** Title asks about newborns but paper doesn't estimate mortality causally.
**Response:** Retitled paper to "Moving Births to Facilities: Evidence from India's National Rural Health Mission." Reframed abstract, introduction, and conclusion to focus on institutional delivery as the primary contribution. Mortality discussion is now explicitly presented as a hypothesis consistent with the evidence, not a demonstrated conclusion. Removed "resolves the disagreement" language.

### 2. "Preferred specification appears selected ex post"
**Concern:** Excluding NE doubles the estimate (15.9 → 25.58).
**Response:** Restructured results to present the all-states estimate (15.89 pp) as the baseline primary result. The EAG-only estimate (25.58 pp) is now presented as heterogeneity analysis showing where the effect is concentrated. Both estimates are reported throughout.

### 3. "Pre-trend evidence is much weaker than paper suggests"
**Concern:** Only 2 of 8 EAG treated states in pre-trend, low power.
**Response:** Replaced all "supported by" and "validated by" language with "suggestive" and "consistent with, though limited in coverage." Abstract now explicitly states "covering 2 of 8 EAG treated states." Added discussion of Roth (2023) power limitations.

### 4. "NFHS-3 baseline contamination"
**Concern:** NFHS-3 includes births exposed to NRHM; direction of bias is ambiguous.
**Response:** Added a dedicated "NFHS-3 baseline contamination" paragraph to Threats to Validity. No longer claim this makes estimates "conservative."

### 5. "Treatment misclassification (J&K, HP)"
**Concern:** Officially treated states coded as controls.
**Response:** Reflects data constraints documented in the text. Acknowledged as limitation.

### 6. "Missing modern DiD citations"
**Concern:** Need Goodman-Bacon, Callaway-Sant'Anna, Sun-Abraham.
**Response:** Added all three citations and a "Staggered treatment concerns" paragraph explaining why classic staggered-bias concerns are mitigated (binary time-invariant treatment, standard 2x2 design).

### 7. "Estimand is narrower than claimed"
**Concern:** Paper estimates differential effect, not total NRHM effect.
**Response:** Added "Estimand interpretation" paragraph. Reframed throughout as "differential effect of early, intensive treatment."

### 8. "State aggregation and equal weighting"
**Response:** Added limitation paragraph acknowledging unweighted design and that population-weighted results could differ.

### 9. "Mechanism claims too strong"
**Response:** Softened all ASHA/JSY attributions. ANC paragraph notes bundled intervention limitation.
