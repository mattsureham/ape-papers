## Verdict: **Conditionally Accept (Minor Revision)**

## Summary

This paper evaluates India's NRHM using five rounds of DHS data and a generalized DiD design. The identification strategy is clean: 18 high-focus states received early/intensive treatment, remaining states received delayed/lower-intensity treatment. The preferred estimate (excl. NE states) is 25.6pp increase in institutional delivery, robust across RI (p=0.007), LOO (23.9–27.5pp range), and continuous treatment specifications. The pre-trend test using 1993–1999 data passes convincingly (p=0.50). The "facility quality paradox" framing is novel and policy-relevant.

## Strengths

1. **Strong identification**: Two pre-treatment periods (1993, 1999) with clean parallel trends test is a genuine advantage over prior NRHM studies.
2. **Robust to everything thrown at it**: RI, LOO, continuous treatment, dose-response — all consistent.
3. **Compelling narrative**: The facility quality paradox reframes the NRHM debate productively.
4. **Clear writing**: Introduction follows the Shleifer arc; results tell a story rather than narrating tables.

## Weaknesses and Required Revisions

### Minor Issues

1. **Table 2 generated by modelsummary**: The column headers and coefficient labels should be checked for LaTeX rendering. Verify the table compiles cleanly in the PDF.

2. **LOO table in Appendix C (Table 7)**: Reports J&K and HP among high-focus states but the text says "preferred specification excluding northeastern states" — clarify that J&K and HP are included in the "excl NE" specification (they are not NE states).

3. **Figure 7 (scatter)**: The x-axis says "Baseline Delivery Rate" but the figure shows JSY intensity on the x-axis. The caption and figure content may not align — verify.

4. **SDE table SD(Y)=23.0**: This should be verified against the actual standard deviation from summary statistics. The summary table reports means with SDs in parentheses — need to confirm 23.0 is the correct unconditional SD of institutional delivery.

5. **Abstract word count**: Verify it's under 150 words.

### Suggestions (Optional)

- The discussion of concurrent policies (Section 4.4) could briefly mention MGNREGA (2005) which also rolled out in phases and could confound.
- The SRS analysis mentioned in the initial plan doesn't appear prominently in the final paper — could strengthen the mortality discussion.

## Decision

The paper is methodologically sound and well-written. The issues above are minor and can be addressed quickly. Recommend proceeding to advisor review after verification fixes.
