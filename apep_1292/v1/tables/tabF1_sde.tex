\begin{tablenotes}
\item \textit{Notes:} Standardized effect size (SDE) = $\hat{\beta} / \sigma_{Y,\text{pre}}$, where $\sigma_{Y,\text{pre}} = 2.625$ is the pre-treatment standard deviation of log(position $+$ 1) in the pooled panel. Classification refers to magnitude, not statistical significance: $|SDE| < 0.2$ (small), $0.2$--$0.5$ (medium), $> 0.5$ (large).
\textbf{Country:} Liechtenstein (as international financial center; reporter countries are 25 BIS-reporting jurisdictions).
\textbf{Research question:} Does bilateral automatic tax information exchange (AEOI) reduce cross-border banking positions with Liechtenstein?
\textbf{Policy mechanism:} AEOI eliminates bank secrecy by requiring automatic reporting of foreign depositor account information to partner tax authorities.
\textbf{Outcome definition:} Log of bilateral banking position (claims or liabilities, millions USD) $+$ 1, measured quarterly.
\textbf{Treatment:} Bilateral AEOI activation between Liechtenstein and reporter country, staggered 2017--2020.
\textbf{Data:} BIS Locational Banking Statistics (LBS), quarterly bilateral cross-border positions, 2010--2023.
\textbf{Method:} Two-way fixed effects (country$\times$position FE $+$ quarter FE); Sun-Abraham for heterogeneous timing.
\textbf{Sample:} 25 countries, 42 country-position pairs, 1,891 observations (2010-Q1 to 2023-Q4).
