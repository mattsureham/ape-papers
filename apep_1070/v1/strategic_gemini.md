## 1. THE ELEVATOR PITCH
The paper examines the massive expansion of the H-2A guestworker program to determine if these foreign workers displace domestic Hispanic farm labor. Using a triple-difference design and a Bartik instrument to isolate demand-driven program growth, it finds that while simple correlations suggest displacement, the causal effect on domestic employment is a precisely estimated zero.

**Evaluation:** The paper articulates this very clearly. The title and the first paragraph of the introduction immediately establish the scale of the policy shift (a quadrupling of the program) and the high-stakes political debate surrounding it. No rewrite is necessary; the "hook" is professionally executed.

---

## 2. CONTRIBUTION CLARITY
**One-sentence contribution:** The paper provides the first causal, administrative-data-based evidence that the rapid expansion of U.S. agricultural guestworkers does not displace domestic labor, but rather serves as a response to existing domestic labor shortages.

**Evaluation:**
- **Differentiation:** It differentiates itself well from the "NAWS-based" literature (e.g., Rutledge et al.) by moving to market-level administrative data (QWI).
- **World vs. Literature:** It frames itself as answering a question about the **world** (does the H-2A program hurt American workers?), which is a core AER strength.
- **Novelty:** A smart economist would see this as more than "another DiD." The "mirage" narrative—explaining *why* OLS is biased through the lens of program rules (the "not sufficient" requirement)—is a compelling insight into selection.
- **Making it bigger:** To truly "max out" the contribution, the paper needs to explore **capital-labor substitution**. If H-2A isn't displacing workers, is it displacing *machines*? Or is it preventing the death of certain crops? Expanding the "So What?" to the survival of the U.S. agricultural sector would elevate it.

---

## 3. LITERATURE POSITIONING
- **Closest Neighbors:** Card (2001), Borjas (2003), Peri & Sparber (2009) on the general immigration/displacement debate; Clemens (2018) and Charlton & Taylor (2019) on agricultural labor supply.
- **Strategy:** The paper "synthesizes" the two. It takes the rigorous identification tools of the modern immigration literature and applies them to the specific, overlooked institutional context of the H-2A program.
- **Missing Literature:** It could benefit from a deeper connection to the **mechanization/automation** literature (e.g., Acemoglu & Restrepo). Is H-2A a bridge to automation or a substitute for it?
- **Conversation:** It is having the right conversation, but it's currently a "Labor" paper. Connecting to "Agricultural Economics" or "Trade" (the alternative to guestworkers is importing the food) would broaden the audience.

---

## 4. NARRATIVE ARC
- **Setup:** A massive influx of foreign farmworkers is entering the U.S.
- **Tension:** Economic theory suggests displacement, and OLS correlations seem to confirm it.
- **Resolution:** The correlation is a "mirage" caused by the fact that the program is legally designed to only function where domestic labor is already disappearing.
- **Implications:** Restricting H-2A won't bring back domestic jobs; it will just leave crops in the field.

**Evaluation:** The narrative arc is exceptionally strong. It follows a "Detective Story" structure (The Clue, The Red Herring, The Truth) which is highly effective for top-tier journals.

---

## 5. THE "SO WHAT?" TEST
- **Dinner Party Fact:** "The program tripled in 4 years, and we still can't find a single domestic worker who lost their job because of it."
- **Reaction:** People will lean in. The H-2A program is a hot-button issue for both labor unions and GOP-leaning farm interests.
- **Follow-up:** "Then why are wages still rising?" or "Is this just delaying the inevitable move of farming to Mexico?"
- **The Null Result:** The null here is the "headline." It isn't a "failed experiment"; it's a "debunking" of a major policy assumption.

---

## 6. STRUCTURAL SUGGESTIONS
- **Front-loading:** The paper is well front-loaded.
- **Mechanism Section:** The "Selection" explanation (Section 5.2) is the most interesting part and could be expanded. I want to see more on the "not sufficient" certification process—maybe some qualitative evidence or data on rejected applications.
- **Appendix:** The "Standardized Effect Sizes" (Table 6) is very helpful for interpreting the null and should stay prominent.

---

## 7. WHAT WOULD MAKE THIS AN AER PAPER?
The science is there, and the story is excellent. The current "gap" is **ambition regarding general equilibrium**. 

If H-2A doesn't displace workers, what *does* it do at the macro level? Does it keep food prices lower? Does it save the rural tax base of the counties that use it? To get into the AER, the author needs to move beyond "no displacement" to "here is the total economic value of this program."

**Single most impactful piece of advice:** Quantify the "Counterfactual Without H-2A"—use your results to estimate how many agricultural jobs (or how much output) would have disappeared entirely if these guestworkers hadn't arrived.
