- **Selected idea title:** Does Aid Stabilize Government Spending? High-Frequency Evidence from Nigeria's Fiscal Shocks
- **Why this is not duplicative:** The aid fungibility literature (Feyzioglu et al. 1998, Van de Walle & Mu 2007, Morrissey 2015) uses annual cross-country panels. No paper has tested within-country, high-frequency fiscal stabilization using administrative daily payment data. The Nigeria Open Treasury dataset is entirely unused in economics research.
- **Specific contribution delta (1-2 sentences):** This paper is the first to use Nigeria's daily government payment data to test whether World Bank aid buffers ministry-level spending during fiscal shocks. The dual-shock design (2020 oil crash + 2023 fuel subsidy removal) tests the mechanism in both directions — stabilization during contraction and absorption during expansion — with built-in placebo groups (health MDAs during COVID, non-aided MDAs).
