### 1. Idea Fidelity
The paper partially pursues the original manifest but misses several key elements. It faithfully uses IPEDS c_a (program survival via positive completions) and hd (sector, location) data, tracks for-profit program mortality around 2015/2019 using a 2011 forward cohort, and incorporates the repeal as a second quasi-experiment in a multi-period DiD. Pre-trends are tested (albeit descriptively). However, it deviates substantially: (i) no merge with DOE GE disclosure files for program-level pass/fail/zone risk scores, eliminating the promised within-institution, cross-program variation and heterogeneity by pre-treatment risk; (ii) no ef_a enrollment outcomes or county-level substitution to public community colleges using hd geolocation; (iii) simplifies to a sector-level (for-profit vs. public) DiD within 2-digit CIP fields with program × year FEs, rather than triple-differences or event study around disclosure dates (2015/2017). These omissions weaken identification and novelty, shifting from program-level risk shocks to a coarser sector comparison.

### 2. Summary
This paper exploits the 2015 Gainful Employment (GE) Rule disclosure and 2019 repeal to estimate large, irreversible declines in for-profit program survival (-29.7 pp post-2015; additional -8.9 pp post-repeal) relative to public programs in the same CIP fields, using IPEDS program-level data and a two-way fixed effects DiD. Surviving programs contract enrollments (-22%), with effects uniform across high- and low-risk CIPs, suggesting a sector-wide "disclosure chill" from reputational damage rather than targeted sanctions. The result highlights asymmetric information effects in regulated markets, with policy relevance for GE reinstatement.

### 3. Essential Points
1. **Parallel trends violation undermines DiD credibility.** The paper transparently notes pre-2015 faster for-profit declines (e.g., placebo -13 pp; survival drops to 62% vs. 83% by 2014), interpreting post estimates as "acceleration," but provides no event-study plot with leads/lags to quantify/ visualize the pre-trend slope or post-2015 break. Without this (standard in AER: Insights DiDs), the -29.7 pp cannot be causally attributed to GE---it conflates trend extrapolation with policy shock. Add a full TWFE event study around 2015 (with 2011-2014 leads) as the primary figure; if pre-trends reject parallelism (p<0.10), reject or switch to synthetic controls/trend-adjusted specs.

2. **Misses core identifying variation from GE disclosures.** Treating all for-profits as uniformly "treated" ignores the manifest's key quasi-experimental variation: program-level pass/fail/zone status from DOE files, which created sharp within-institution shocks (e.g., cosmetology vs. healthcare). Current sector DiD cannot distinguish disclosure from sanction or sector stigma; heterogeneity (similar effects for low-risk CIPs) is suggestive but post-hoc. Merge disclosures by OPEID/CIP/award_level (public data), re-estimate as \(\beta\) (Fail/Zone \(\times\) Post-Disclosure) with institution-CIP FEs, and triple-diff vs. public. Without this, results are correlational, not causal on "disclosure chill."

3. **Clustering and SEs inappropriately narrow given structure.** State-level clustering (59 clusters) yields implausibly precise SEs (1.0 pp on binary outcome with 2.5M obs, 181k programs), but serial correlation is likely within institutions (3k-4k units) or CIP-fields (39 fields), and program FEs induce incidental parameters bias with many short panels (exiting programs). Magnitudes are plausible (aligns with 46% drop), but SEs undervalue uncertainty. Two-way cluster at (state, institution) or use wild bootstrap; report if results hold.

### 4. Suggestions
**Strengthen visualization and diagnostics (priority for Insights format).** Replace \Cref{tab:main} leads with an event-study plot (e.g., 2011=lead-4 to 2023) as Figure 1, binning post periods (2015-17, 2018-19, 2020+) if needed. Test pre-trends formally (joint F-test on leads=0) and interact trends with for-profit share or CIP-risk. Add dynamic DiD for repeal (post-2019 leads/lags). These would make the "clear break at 2015" claim credible and elevate to publishable.

**Incorporate promised data for sharper ID and heterogeneity.** Download GE files (FSA IFAP, ~800 failing programs 2015; ~98 post-appeals), merge to IPEDS (high feasibility per manifest), and estimate treatment intensity: e.g., \(Y_{ipt} = \sum_k \beta_k (RiskScore_{ip} \times Post_k) + \alpha_{ic} + \delta_t\), with RiskScore = fail/zone/pass (or continuous D/E ratio). This exploits within-institution variation (e.g., healthcare pass vs. cosmetology fail at same school), falsifies sanction channel (pass programs shouldn't exit much), and tests spillovers (pass \(\beta <0\)?). Subsample to institutions with mixed programs. For substitution, geocode hd lat/long to counties, regress public CC enrollment growth on lagged for-profit share \(\times\) PostGE (ef_a data ready).

**Refine outcomes and extensives.** Survival via positive completions is creative but noisy (dormant programs?); validate against hd closures or ef_a enrollment=0. Add program births (new entrants post-2015) to test market restructuring. Intensive margin: log(1+completions) avoids survivor bias; winsorize outliers. State-level substitution is underpowered---switch to county/CIP panel with hd geocodes (e.g., \(\Delta PublicEnroll_{ct} = \gamma (ForProfitShare_{c,pre} \times PostGE_t) + \controls\)). Standardized effects (\Cref{tab:sde}) are excellent; extend to heterogeneity table.

**Address confounders and mechanisms rigorously.** Tabulate timeline of confounders (CFPB suits, Corinthian/ITT closures 2015-16, PSD2 2016) by state/CIP, interact in robustness (e.g., high-AG-activity states). Mechanism tests: (i) media coverage (Google Trends "gainful employment [CIP]"); (ii) enrollment by student demographics (IPEDS if available); (iii) re-entry barriers (regression discontinuity at accreditation cycles?). Broaden discussion: link irreversibility to Dixit-Pindyck entry costs; compare to nursing home report cards (irreversible exits).

**Polish presentation for AER: Insights (4-8k words).** Trim intro (puzzle vivid but long); move summary stats to appendix. Table 1: add means/pre-trends column; column (3) CIP-level aggregation loses power---weight consistently. Robustness: add matched sample (PSM on pre-trends/institution size). Appendix: full balance table (CIP shares, size); power calcs (detect 5pp at 80% power?). JEL/keywords spot-on; cite more disclosure papers (e.g., Hastings-Greenstein 2023 on school ratings persistence). Overall, magnitudes economically meaningful (halves sector), result clear if ID fixed---strong potential with revisions.
