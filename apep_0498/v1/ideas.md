# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: The Austerity Mortality Gradient: Public Health Grant Cuts and Deaths of Despair in England
**Policy:** Ring-fenced public health grant to English upper-tier local authorities, introduced April 2013 when public health functions transferred from NHS Primary Care Trusts to local government. Between 2015/16 and 2024/25, real per-capita grants were cut by ~24% nationally, but cuts were distributed unevenly across LAs — historically well-funded (often more deprived) areas saw larger real-terms reductions as the government moved toward a needs-based formula.
**Outcome:** Deaths of despair (drug misuse mortality, alcohol-specific mortality) and under-75 preventable mortality from the OHID Fingertips API (indicators 92432, 91380, 108). Annual LA-level data available from 2001 onward.
**Identification:** Continuous treatment-intensity DiD. Treatment = change in real per-capita public health grant (deflated by GDP deflator). Pre-period: 2006-2012 (health data under PCT-era NHS, before LA devolution). Post-period: 2013-2024. ~150 upper-tier LAs provide ample cluster variation. Use formula-determined grant allocations from DHSC exposition books (exogenous to individual LA health trajectories, determined by central needs-based formula). Callaway-Sant'Anna with binarized treatment cohorts (terciles of grant reduction) + continuous treatment TWFE as robustness.
**Why it's novel:** Existing work (Alexiou et al. 2021 Lancet PH; Martin et al. 2021 SSM-PH) uses total local government spending (not the ring-fenced public health grant), covers only 2013-2017, and uses basic FE/cross-sectional IV — no staggered DiD, no deaths-of-despair focus, no HonestDiD sensitivity. Martin et al. explicitly note they "cannot do panel estimation." This paper fills that gap with modern methods and 10+ year panel.
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: (1) Grant data downloadable from GOV.UK (12 annual exposition books, per-LA amounts). (2) Fingertips API returns LA-level drug misuse deaths, alcohol-specific mortality, and under-75 mortality — tested and confirmed. (3) ~150 upper-tier LAs with data from 2001. (4) Not in APEP list. (5) Limited rigorous causal work.
**Mechanism chain:** Grant cut → reduced drug/alcohol treatment capacity (testable: NDTMS treatment completions via Fingertips indicator 90244) → fewer people in recovery → more drug/alcohol deaths. Built-in placebo: road traffic deaths (not affected by public health budgets); cancer mortality (long latency, unresponsive to short-term spending changes).

## Idea 2: Business Improvement Districts and Urban Safety: Staggered Evidence from England
**Policy:** Business Improvement Districts (BIDs) — geographic areas where businesses vote for a mandatory levy (typically 1-2% of rateable value) to fund local improvements (security patrols, cleaning, marketing). England has had BIDs since 2004 with 300+ now operational, each starting at a specific ballot date.
**Outcome:** Street-level crime from UK Police API (by LSOA-month, 2010-2024), property values from Land Registry PPD.
**Identification:** Staggered DiD across 300+ BIDs with different activation dates. Control: areas just outside BID boundaries within the same LA, or BID proposals that were voted down (failed ballots as clean control).
**Why it's novel:** No rigorous causal evidence on UK BIDs. Most studies are descriptive. The global BID literature has a handful of US papers (Brooks 2008, Hoyt 2005) but nothing with modern staggered DiD and the scale of the UK BID program.
**Feasibility check:** PARTIALLY confirmed. BID activation dates exist in the British BIDs database, but accessibility as a downloadable dataset is uncertain. Boundary polygons would need GIS compilation. Crime data confirmed via Police API. Land Registry confirmed. Risk: data compilation effort may be high.

## Idea 3: When the Waters Rise: Flood Events, Property Markets, and the Persistence of Risk Mispricing
**Policy:** Plausibly exogenous flood shocks across England, identified via Environment Agency flood warning data (severity 1-4 warnings with dates and geographic areas).
**Outcome:** Property transaction prices from Land Registry PPD (24M+ transactions, postcode-level, 1995-2024). Repeat-sales design within postcodes.
**Identification:** Event study / DiD around major flood events. Compare property values in flood-affected LSOAs vs matched non-affected LSOAs before and after flood events. Multiple events (2013-14 coastal, 2015 Storm Desmond, 2020 Ciara/Dennis) provide replication across settings.
**Why it's novel:** Limited rigorous evidence on flood risk capitalization in England. The introduction of Flood Re (2016, reinsurance scheme capping flood premiums) provides a structural break — do flood events still move prices after insurance risk is socialized? This tests the efficient markets hypothesis for flood risk.
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: (1) EA flood monitoring API returns station-level flood warnings. (2) Land Registry PPD bulk download confirmed. (3) Spatial matching via postcodes.io or ONS NSPL. Risk: defining "flood-affected" precisely requires careful spatial work.

## Idea 4: The Price of Clean Air: Business Displacement Effects of Urban Clean Air Zones
**Policy:** Clean Air Zones (CAZ) in English cities — Bath (Mar 2021), Birmingham (Jun 2021), Portsmouth (Nov 2021), Bristol (Nov 2022), Bradford (Sep 2022), Sheffield (Feb 2023), plus London ULEZ expansions (2019, 2021, 2023).
**Outcome:** Firm entry/exit by SIC code from Companies House bulk data; property values from Land Registry PPD.
**Identification:** Staggered DiD across 6-8 cities. Within-city comparison: LSOAs inside vs outside CAZ boundary. Decompose by industry: transport-dependent SIC codes (logistics, construction, trades) vs transport-independent (finance, IT, professional services).
**Why it's novel:** Most CAZ research focuses on pollution/health. The economic displacement question — do CAZs push out blue-collar businesses? — is understudied.
**Feasibility check:** PARTIAL. Only 6-8 treated cities — below the ≥20 treated units DiD rule for cluster-level inference. Within-city LSOA-level analysis provides more treated units but cluster inference at city level is problematic. CAZ boundary polygons available from council websites but not centralized. RISKY due to low N of treated clusters.
