 **Referee Report: "Still Short-Staffed: Healthcare Employment and the Enhanced Nurse Licensure Compact"**

**1. Idea Fidelity**

The paper delivers on the core promise of evaluating the Enhanced Nurse Licensure Compact (eNLC) using employer-side Quarterly Workforce Indicators (QWI) and staggered difference-in-differences methods. The analysis of NAICS 621/622/623 subsectors and the triple-difference robustness check represent faithful execution of the original design. However, the paper omits three key elements from the manifest: (i) the **border-county spatial regression discontinuity**, which would have provided sharper identification for the mobility mechanism central to the policy; (ii) systematic integration of **IPEDS nursing completions data** to test the "training pipeline constraint" hypothesis that drives the paper's interpretation; and (iii) the promised **education decomposition (E3 vs. E4)** as a primary analysis rather than a brief appendix mention. These omissions weaken the ability to distinguish between a true null effect and limitations in the measurement model.
