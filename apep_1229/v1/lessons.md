## Discovery
- **Idea selected:** idea_2185 — FCA GIPP loyalty penalty ban and insurance competition, chosen for sharp institutional shock and portable "convergence trap" mechanism
- **Data source:** ONS CPIH detailed reference tables (Table 37, 458 months) + FCA GI Value Measures 2024 (product-level loss ratios). Older FCA files (2017-2019) had different structures and only covered home products. BoE insurance aggregate data was solvency/investment, not useful.
- **Key risk:** Pre-trends were not parallel (transport insurance was diverging from health insurance before GIPP). Claims inflation concurrent with GIPP makes causal attribution impossible.

## Execution
- **What worked:** ONS CPIH data is excellent — monthly frequency, 38-year history, disaggregated by insurance type. The 83% surge in transport insurance is a striking descriptive fact.
- **What didn't:** The DiD identification failed (placebo test significant). FCA data only available for 2023-2024 (no pre-treatment loss ratios). The convergence trap mechanism was reframed from "margin expansion" to "reduced demand elasticity enabling fuller cost pass-through" after reviewers correctly noted that stable loss ratios contradict the coordination story.
- **Review feedback adopted:** Reframed DiD as descriptive divergence (not causal); noted Newey-West SEs needed for serial correlation; clarified that stable loss ratios = cost pass-through, not margin expansion; softened all causal language in abstract and discussion.
