## Discovery
- **Idea selected:** idea_1593 — China anti-corruption campaign and fiscal composition
- **Data source:** Wang (2020) Harvard Dataverse + China City Yearbook (Harvard Dataverse)
- **Key risk:** NBS API was completely down; pivoted to Dataverse-hosted city yearbook panel

## Execution
- **What worked:** The continuous-intensity design was the right call given near-universal treatment (253/258 cities). The "compliance shift" toward science spending was a genuine surprise — more interesting than either confirming or simply nullifying Mauro.
- **What didn't:** FAI/GDP is too broad a proxy for government infrastructure spending (includes private investment). Health expenditure was unavailable in the City Statistical Yearbook, limiting the scope of the Mauro test. The Callaway-Sant'Anna design was weak given limited timing variation and few never-treated units.
- **Review feedback adopted:** Moderated causal language throughout to "suggestive"/"associated with"; corrected effect size units (0.16 pp, not 0.0016 pp); expanded limitations section substantially to address endogeneity of intensity, FAI proxy concerns, and alternative explanations for the compliance shift. Retained the finding but positioned it honestly as a descriptive pattern requiring further investigation, not a clean causal estimate.
