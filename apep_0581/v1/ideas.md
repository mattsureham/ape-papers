# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: Technology Standards as Industrial Policy: BAT Conclusions and Facility-Level Pollution Under the EU Industrial Emissions Directive
**Policy:** EU Industrial Emissions Directive (2010/75/EU) requires ~52,000 installations to operate according to Best Available Techniques (BAT) conclusions adopted through the "Sevilla Process." BAT conclusions are staggered by sector: Iron/Steel (Mar 2012), Glass (Mar 2012), Cement (Mar 2013), Pulp/Paper (Sep 2014), Refining (Oct 2014), Non-Ferrous Metals (Jun 2016), Large Combustion Plants (Aug 2017), Waste Treatment (Aug 2018), Food/Drink (Dec 2019). Order determined by inherited BREF review schedule, not sectoral dirtiness.
**Outcome:** Primary: Facility-level pollutant releases (NOx, SOx, CO2, PM10, heavy metals) from EEA Industrial Reporting database/E-PRTR. 854,731 facility-year observations, 34,000+ facilities, 91 pollutants, 65 economic activities. Secondary: Eurostat SBS sector-level outcomes (turnover, employment, investment) for IED-regulated NACE sectors.
**Identification:** Staggered DiD across sectors. Treatment: BAT conclusion publication date + 4-year compliance deadline. 14+ sector cohorts, 2012-2019. Identifying assumption: emission trends parallel across sectors absent BAT conclusions. Sun-Abraham or CS-DiD for heterogeneity robustness. Triple-difference: IED vs sub-threshold facilities in same sector. Single-sector site restriction for cross-sector spillover test.
**Why it's novel:** Despite IED affecting 52,000 installations since 2011, no rigorous causal evaluation exists. E-PRTR data used descriptively by EEA but never for causal inference. The staggered sector design provides a textbook natural experiment. No equivalent in US literature for EU's technology-based approach.
**Feasibility check:** Variation: confirmed — 14+ sector cohorts staggered over 8 years. Data access: confirmed — EEA ArcGIS returns 854,731 facility observations; SBS 455 values for 6 IED sectors. Novelty: confirmed — no causal study of IED BAT conclusions. Sample size: 34,000+ facilities/year x 16 years.
