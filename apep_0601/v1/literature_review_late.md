## Literature Coverage Assessment

The paper engages with the relevant literature on FDA review timing and drug safety:

### Core Citations (Present)
- **Carpenter (2010)** — FDA reputation and organizational behavior under PDUFA
- **Downing et al. (2017)** — Correlational evidence on post-market safety of PDUFA-era drugs
- **Carpenter et al. (2008)** — PDUFA and review speed effects
- **Calonico et al. (2014)** — RD methodology (rdrobust)
- **Cattaneo et al. (2020)** — Density testing (rddensity)
- **Saez (2010), Kleven (2016)** — Bunching estimator methodology
- **Lee & Lemieux (2008)** — RD identification with imprecise manipulation

### Positioning
The paper is positioned at the intersection of:
1. **Economics of regulation** — regulatory deadline effects on bureaucratic behavior
2. **Drug safety** — post-market surveillance and FAERS-based outcomes
3. **Bunching estimation** — applying public finance methods to regulatory settings

The introduction clearly identifies the gap: prior work (Downing et al.) showed correlations between review timing and safety, but lacked quasi-experimental variation. This paper provides the first bunching-based identification strategy for this question.

### Potential Gaps (Minor)
- Could cite Olson (2004) on FDA organizational incentives more prominently
- Berndt et al. (2005) discussion could be expanded
- No engagement with the broader "bureaucratic performance" literature (e.g., Besley & Ghatak)

These are minor and do not warrant a REVISE status for a V1 (AER: Insights) paper.
