# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: When the Canal Runs Dry: Trade Costs and Port Diversion from the 2023-24 Panama Canal Drought
**Policy:** The Panama Canal Authority imposed escalating transit restrictions from July 2023 through February 2024 due to historically low water levels in Gatun Lake driven by El Nino. Daily transits fell from 36-38 (normal) to 18 (February 2024). Draft restrictions reduced cargo per vessel. Restrictions eased gradually from January 2024, with full capacity restored by August 2024. The drought was driven by El Nino, not policy choice — a pure supply shock to transit capacity.
**Outcome:** Monthly port-level merchandise imports from US Census Bureau International Trade Data API (api.census.gov/data/timeseries/intltrade/imports/porths). Freely accessible, no API key required. Covers ~100 US customs districts/ports, monthly, by country of origin and HS commodity code. Secondary: BTS Port Performance Freight Statistics (monthly TEUs), Henry Hub natural gas spot prices from FRED (DHHNGSP), EIA LNG trade data.
**Identification:** Continuous Treatment DiD exploiting differential Panama Canal dependence across ~35 major US ports. East Coast ports (Savannah, Charleston, Norfolk, Baltimore) receive ~30-50% of Asian imports via Canal. West Coast ports (LA, Long Beach, Seattle, Oakland) receive Asian imports via direct trans-Pacific — zero Canal dependence. Strategy 2: Origin-country x Port interaction absorbs port-level demand and country-level supply shocks. Strategy 3: Port diversion test — West Coast ports should see increased volumes during drought. El Nino drought exogenous to US port conditions. Parallel trends testable with 2019-2023 data.
**Why it's novel:** No econometric paper estimates causal trade diversion effects of the Panama Canal drought. Feyrer (2021, REStud) studies 1967 Suez closure at country-pair level. Brancaccio et al. (2020, AER) model shipping networks without shocks. The Panama drought is unique: genuinely exogenous (weather), recent with granular monthly port data, clean East/Gulf vs West Coast treatment-control contrast, gradual (dose-response) and temporary (recovery dynamics). Provides first port-level trade diversion elasticity with climate adaptation implications.
**Feasibility check:** Variation confirmed — daily transits fell 50% (36→18). East Coast ports 30-50% Canal-dependent; West Coast 0%. Data access confirmed — Census port-level import API returns monthly data. Novelty confirmed — no causal paper on 2023-24 Panama Canal drought. Sample: ~35 major ports x 18 months x ~200 origin countries.
