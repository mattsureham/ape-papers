## Discovery
- **Policy chosen:** US civil asset forfeiture reform (34 jurisdictions, 2014-2019) → drug overdose mortality — novel welfare outcome for a well-identified policy shock
- **Ideas rejected:** Korea 52-hour/fertility (COVID kills waves 2-3), Japan dual-rate tax (short post-treatment), sports betting/lottery (data assembly manual), UK police austerity (fewer treated units)
- **Data source:** CDC NCHS Drug Poisoning (2004-2015) + VSRR Provisional (2016-2019); Census ACS population; IJ forfeiture data
- **Key risk:** Dual data source transition in middle of treatment window; limited post-treatment periods for late cohorts; Virginia 2020 reform outside estimation window

## Review
- **Advisor verdict:** PASS on round 6 (3/3 available advisors). First 5 rounds failed on sample count inconsistencies, pre-treatment coefficient counts, significance stars, placeholder text
- **Top criticism:** Both referees flagged concurrent opioid policy confounding (naloxone, PDMP, Medicaid expansion) as the primary threat to identification
- **Surprise feedback:** Referees strongly objected to welfare calculation extrapolating Minnesota-only e+5 estimate to all jurisdictions
- **What changed:** Major claim recalibration — reframed from "evidence of benefit" to "absence of harm"; welfare calc shortened; heterogeneity labeled exploratory

## Summary
- **Total advisor rounds:** 6 (many due to cascading count inconsistencies from Virginia 2020 exclusion)
- **Key lesson:** When excluding units from estimation window, trace ALL downstream count references exhaustively
- **Framing lesson:** Null/imprecise results should lead with "no evidence of harm" framing, not suggestive benefit claims
