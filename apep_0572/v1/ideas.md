# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: The Anatomy of Import Compression: How Egypt's 2016 Devaluation Reshaped Trade Along the Value Chain
**Policy:** Egypt's 48% overnight EGP devaluation (November 3, 2016), when the Central Bank of Egypt abandoned the managed exchange rate of ~8.8 EGP/USD as a condition of a $12 billion IMF Extended Fund Facility. The pound fell to ~18 EGP/USD by early 2017, mechanically doubling the local-currency cost of all dollar-denominated imports overnight.
**Outcome:** UN COMTRADE HS6-level bilateral annual and monthly trade data for Egypt (reporter code 818). ~5,000 HS6 products per year classified by BEC Rev.5 into intermediate goods, capital goods, and final consumption goods. 2010-2023 (13 years, 6 pre-treatment, 7 post-treatment). With monthly data: ~780,000 product-month observations.
**Identification:** Product-level event study with continuous treatment intensity based on BEC end-use classification. The key variation is across products classified by their position in the production chain: intermediate inputs (inelastic demand — firms need them to produce), capital goods (deferrable investment), and final consumption goods (consumers can substitute or reduce). Product and time fixed effects absorb level differences and common shocks. Built-in placebo: capital goods should show intermediate response between intermediates and finals. Triple-difference extension: dollar-denominated vs. euro-denominated partner sources.
**Why it's novel:** Existing literature on Egypt's 2016 devaluation covers inflation/cost-of-living (Arezki et al. 2025, JAfrE), firm-level export margins (ECES WP 198), and export response. No study examines the import composition response — which imports survive and which collapse. First clean causal test of the "import hierarchy" hypothesis at the product level. Contributes to exchange rate pass-through literature (Goldberg & Knetter 1997, Amiti et al. 2014) and imported inputs and growth (Halpern et al. 2015, Goldberg et al. 2010).
**Feasibility check:** Variation confirmed (48% devaluation overnight, ~5,000 HS6 products). Data access confirmed (UN COMTRADE public API returns Egypt HS2/HS6 data monthly and annually; BEC concordance publicly available). Novelty confirmed (no product-level import composition study exists). Sample size: ~65,000 product-year or ~780,000 product-month observations.
