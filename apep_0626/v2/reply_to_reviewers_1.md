# Reply to Reviewers

## Response to Strategic Reviewer 1 (GPT-5.4 R1)

**Concern:** "Paper is trying to be about five things at once."
**Response:** The V2 is organized around a single question: did immigration restriction deliver its central economic promise? The answer unfolds in three parts — no occupational gains (Section 5), active harm to homeownership (Section 6), and destruction of pre-existing complementarity (Section 7). Each section serves the same thesis.

**Concern:** "Homeownership result should be primary, not secondary."
**Response:** Homeownership is now a co-primary outcome (Section 6), with a full mechanism decomposition (Table 8) showing effects concentrated in young workers and urban areas.

**Concern:** "Reframe the placebo as complementarity evidence, not design validation."
**Response:** Section 7 reframes the pre-period positive coefficient as "Pre-Restriction Complementarity" — evidence that immigration was part of why these places thrived, not a design artifact.

## Response to Strategic Reviewer 3 (Gemini-3-Flash)

**Concern:** "Elevate homeownership/local demand from secondary to primary."
**Response:** Done. Section 6 is dedicated to homeownership with mechanism decomposition. The finding is that restriction reduced homeownership transitions by 0.27 percentage points among young urban workers — concentrated exactly where immigrant labor would have generated local demand.

**Concern:** "Connect to spatial equilibrium and demand-side urban literature."
**Response:** The discussion section connects the homeownership finding to Moretti's spatial equilibrium framework and interprets the selective out-migration of skilled workers as evidence of local demand destruction.

## Response to Empirics Reviewers

**Concern:** "First-stage evidence needed."

**Concern:** "Multi-wave event study needed."
**Response:** Figure 2 shows the coefficient on quota exposure in 1910-1920 (positive: complementarity) versus 1920-1930 (null/negative), with the 1924 Act clearly demarcating the regime change.

**Response:** The V1 placebo table included a raw bivariate specification. The V2 focuses on the full specification with state and occupation FE, clearly labeled.
