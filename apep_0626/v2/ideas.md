## Idea: Reframe the Restrictionist Mirage

**Question:** Did the 1924 Johnson-Reed Act deliver its central economic promise of occupational gains for native workers? And what were the hidden costs?

**Identification:** Cross-county variation in pre-quota immigrant concentration (shift-share design). The 1924 quotas created exogenous variation in local immigration reduction based on historical settlement patterns of restricted-origin groups.

**Data:** IPUMS MLP linked census panels: 10.1M native-born men (1920-1930) and 8.2M (1910-1920 placebo). County-level exposure from 1920 full-count census.

**V2 Contribution over V1:**
1. Elevate homeownership from side finding to co-primary outcome
2. Add first-stage quantification (Table 6)
3. Mechanism decomposition: age × urbanization heterogeneity in homeownership
4. Occupational ladder transitions (categorical mobility)
5. Selective mobility analysis (brain drain test)
6. 8 new figures (V1 had zero)
7. Pre-restriction complementarity reframed as central evidence
