# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: The Resilience Puzzle: How European Manufacturing Survived the Russian Gas Shock
**Policy:** Russia's 2022 gas cutoff to Europe — pipeline deliveries fell from ~155 bcm (2021) to near zero (2023). Country-level pre-war Russian gas dependence ranges from 0% (Norway) to ~80% (Hungary). TTF gas price spiked 11x from ~30 to 342 EUR/MWh. Key dates: Feb 24 invasion, Jun-Sep 2022 progressive Nord Stream shutdown, Sep 26 pipeline sabotage.
**Outcome:** Eurostat STS_INPR_M (monthly industrial production, ~25 EU countries × ~20 NACE sectors × 48+ months). Supplemented with: (a) Eurostat producer prices (STS_INPPD_M) for energy cost pass-through, (b) Eurostat import/export data (DS-045409 or STS trade indicators) for trade reallocation, (c) Bruegel/EU State Aid tracker for government energy subsidies by country, (d) Eurostat NRG_TI_GAS for LNG import expansion. All confirmed accessible via Eurostat public API.
**Identification:** Continuous-treatment DiD: Y_{c,s,t} = α_{cs} + γ_{ct} + δ_{st} + β × GasExposure_c × GasIntensity_s × Post_t + ε. Triple fixed effects (country×sector, country×month, sector×month) absorb country-level macro shocks, global sector trends, and time-invariant country-sector differences. The key coefficient β captures whether MORE gas-exposed countries experienced LARGER production declines in MORE gas-intensive sectors. Novel framing: the null/small β from apep_0544 becomes the FINDING — the paper explains WHY through mechanism decomposition: (1) fiscal subsidies attenuated cost pass-through in high-subsidy countries, (2) LNG substitution reduced effective exposure, (3) import reallocation shifted production to less-exposed countries. Placebo: electricity-intensive but gas-independent sectors should show no effect.
**Why it's novel:** Prior work (Bachmann et al. 2022 Econometrica, Moll et al. 2023) predicted 0.2-3% GDP losses from Russian gas cutoff via CGE/structural models. The "energy catastrophe" narrative dominated 2022-23 policy. This paper provides the first ex-post causal test and finds the predicted collapse largely didn't happen — then explains why. This reframes from "damage estimation" (apep_0544's failed approach) to "resilience decomposition," answering the bigger question: can advanced economies survive weaponized energy dependence? Contributes to strategic dependence literature (Farrell & Newman 2019), energy-manufacturing nexus (Allcott et al. 2016), and fiscal stabilization (Blanchard et al. 2010).
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: Eurostat STS_INPR_M returns 1,368+ data points per query (tested in idea manifest). Russian gas shares range 0-80% across 25+ countries. Gas intensity varies 6x across manufacturing sectors. Bruegel energy subsidy tracker provides country-level fiscal response data. LNG import data available from Eurostat NRG_TI_GAS. Sample: ~25 countries × 20 sectors × 48 months ≈ 24,000 obs.
