# Research Ideas

## Idea 1: Walls Without Bricks: Temporary Schengen Border Controls and Regional Economic Activity in European Border Regions
**Policy:** Since September 2015, six Schengen members reintroduced temporary internal border controls: Germany-Austria (Sep 13, 2015, ongoing); Austria-Hungary/Slovenia (Sep 16, 2015); Sweden-Denmark/Oresund (Nov 12, 2015); Norway-ferry routes (Nov 26, 2015); France all borders (Nov 13, 2015); Denmark-Germany (Jan 4, 2016). Intended as temporary but serially renewed for a decade. Selective: e.g., Germany-Netherlands and Austria-Italy initially uncontrolled.
**Outcome:** Primary: NUTS3 GDP (nama_10r_3gdp, 2003-2024), GVA by sector (nama_10r_3gva), employment (nama_10r_3empers), road freight (road_go_na_rl3g). Secondary: cross-border commuter flows (lfst_r_lfe2ecomm), tourism nights (tour_occ_nin2).
**Identification:** Staggered DiD comparing treated NUTS3 border regions to: (a) unaffected Schengen border regions (Germany-Netherlands, Austria-Italy); (b) interior NUTS3 regions. 12+ pre-treatment years (2003-2014). Callaway-Sant'Anna with 6+ border-segment treatment cohorts. Refugee crisis addressed via placebo on non-migration-route borders.
**Why it's novel:** Felbermayr et al. (2018 JCMS) study Schengen's trade effects via gravity models for the original OPENING. Nobody has studied the REVERSAL — the re-imposition since 2015 using NUTS3 regional data. Tests whether integration gains are reversible or hysteresis protects border economies.
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: ~80-100 treated NUTS3 border regions, 6+ treatment cohorts, 12+ pre-treatment years, data accessible via Eurostat API with no keys needed.
